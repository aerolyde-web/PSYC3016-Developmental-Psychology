1. TWO-PATHWAY HYPOTHESIS: The Unifying Framework

Core Question: How do infants understand other minds exist?
Answer: Two INDEPENDENT pathways developing asynchronously, selectively impaired in different populations.
  • Implicit ToM (System 1): Fast, automatic, unconscious. Measured via looking-time/VoE paradigms. Emerges ~15 months. Onishi & Baillargeon (2005): 15-mo look longer at belief-incongruent actions (M=39.3s vs 29.1s, Δ=10.2s). Shared with great apes—phylogenetically ancient. Neural: mirror neuron system (inferior parietal, premotor).
  • Explicit ToM (System 2): Slow, deliberate, conscious. Measured via verbal prediction tasks (Sally-Anne). Emerges ~4-5 years. Requires language + working memory + inhibitory control to override reality bias. ~85% of 4yr pass vs ~20% of 3yr. Uniquely human. Neural: mentalizing network (mPFC, TPJ, precuneus).
  • Independence Proof: Pathways are computationally INDEPENDENT—not developmental stages. The 3-year gap (15mo→4yr) reflects PFC maturation enabling inhibitory control, NOT regression. Damage to one pathway leaves other intact. Double dissociation: Apes (implicit✓/explicit✗) vs HFASD (implicit✗/explicit✓).
Why 3-year gap (15mo→4yr)? NOT regression—SEPARATE systems! Implicit=automatic, ape-shared, mirror neurons. Explicit=deliberate, human-unique, requires EF. Double dissociation proves independence: apes pass implicit/fail explicit; HFASD pass explicit/fail implicit.

2. DEVELOPMENTAL PRECURSORS: Building Blocks Timeline

  • 9 months — 9-month revolution: Dyadic→triadic attention. 5 milestones: gaze following, joint engagement, referential gestures, imitation, intention understanding. Carpenter: r=.52 JA at 9mo → language at 24mo. Failure = ASD risk marker.
  • 12 months — Social referencing: Use others' emotions as information. Sorce visual cliff: 0% crossed (fear) vs 74% (joy). Liszkowski selective informing: 2.1:1 ratio pointing to uninformed adult. Infants track who saw what.
  • 15 months — Implicit false-belief: Onishi & Baillargeon VoE 39.3s vs 29.1s. Expect belief-consistent action without verbal response. "15-month-olds expect actions consistent with beliefs."
  • 18-24 months — Self-recognition: Rouge test ~50% at 18mo → ~70% by 24mo. Desire understanding: Repacholi & Gopnik broccoli task—18mo give experimenter's preference (broccoli), 14mo give own (crackers). Desire precedes belief.
  • 18 months — Uninformed vs Misinformed: Liszkowski: Infants point MORE for misinformed (85%) than uninformed (30%)—they correct false beliefs before passing Sally-Anne!
Sequence: 9mo triadic (r=.52→lang) → 12mo referencing (0%/74% cliff; 2.1:1 informing) → 15mo implicit FB (39.3s/29.1s) → 18mo self+desire (broccoli) → 4yr explicit. Each builds on previous; early failure predicts ASD.

5. TELEOLOGICAL REASONING & BELIEF-DESIRE FRAMEWORK

  • Teleological Reasoning (9mo): Interpreting actions in terms of goals and efficiency—understanding agents choose rational means. Csibra Mummy-Ball: Infants expect efficient, rational action, not mere repetition. This is "lean" attribution: goals WITHOUT beliefs. Precursor to full ToM because you must represent goals before you can represent beliefs ABOUT means to goals.
  • Wellman's Belief-Desire Framework: To predict action, know BOTH desire (what they want) AND belief (what they think). Desire emerges first (~18mo) because desires are always "true"—goal states that motivate action. Beliefs can be FALSE—require meta-representation (mind can misrepresent reality). This is why belief understanding is harder and emerges ~2-3 years later.
  • Perception-Knowledge Link: "Seeing leads to knowing." Liszkowski shows 12-mo track who saw what (selectivity 2.1:1). By 18mo, distinguish uninformed (never saw) from misinformed (saw wrong location): 85% point for misinformed vs 30% for uninformed—infants correct false beliefs implicitly before passing Sally-Anne!
Why Desire Before Belief? Desires = goal states (always "true," motivate action). Beliefs = representations of reality (can be FALSE). Understanding false belief requires meta-representation: grasping that mind can represent world incorrectly.
Teleological (9mo)→Desire (18mo)→Belief (4yr). Each cognitively more demanding. Desire always "true"; beliefs can be FALSE. 18mo correct false beliefs (85% vs 30%) before passing Sally-Anne—implicit FB before explicit!

  • ASD-DS Double Dissociation: If ToM = general intelligence, higher-IQ ASD should outperform lower-IQ DS. Opposite result → ToM is domain-specific, MODULAR, not reducible to "being smart about people." Dedicated cognitive architecture selectively impaired (ASD) or spared (DS).

7. SPECIES COMPARISONS: Human vs Ape & Shared Intentionality

CapacityHumansApesEvidence & Implication
Implicit ToMYes (15mo)YesCall, Buttelmann: Apes pass VoE paradigms = implicit ToM phylogenetically ancient.
Explicit ToMYes (4yr)NoApes fail ALL verbal/communicative ToM tasks. Requires language + cultural learning.
Informational pointingYes (12mo)NoLiszkowski: Humans point to SHARE info (declarative); apes point only to REQUEST (imperative).
Shared intentionalityYesNoTomasello "we-mode": Joint goals, mutual knowledge, collaborative roles. Foundation of culture.
  • Tomasello's Shared Intentionality: Humans establish common ground—mutual knowledge that we are jointly engaged. "We-mode" vs individual intentionality. Enables language, morality, institutions. Apes have individual intentionality but NOT "we" psychology.
  • Ape limitation: Apes collaborate only when EACH action independently yields reward. Humans collaborate with complementary roles toward shared goal. Human 12-mo point declaratively to share; chimps never point to inform, only to request.
  • Neural basis for uniqueness: Explicit ToM requires mentalizing network (mPFC, TPJ) which is elaborated in humans. Mirror system (implicit) is shared with primates. PFC maturation enables inhibitory control for explicit ToM at ~4yr.
Apes share implicit ToM but lack explicit + shared intentionality. Human uniqueness = "we-mode" (Tomasello): joint attention, informational pointing, common ground. Foundation of cultural cognition. 12mo humans point declaratively; chimps never do.
