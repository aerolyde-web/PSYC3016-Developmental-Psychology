LO1-A Mirror Self-Recognition: The Rouge Test
"Describe the Rouge test methodology and explain what self-recognition reveals about social-cognitive development..."
# Component Details Statistical Anchor
1 Methodology
Procedure		 (1) Baseline: count face-touching frequency. (2) Mark face with rouge while distracting (tactilely undetectable). (3) Present mirror. (4) Score: mark-directed touches > baseline = PASS Mark-specificity ratio > 1.0
2 Human Development
Age Progression		 Gradual emergence. Requires linking external image to internal embodied self. Touch OWN face, not mirror 18mo: ~25%
24mo: 50%
36mo: ~90%
3 Great Ape Comparison
Cross-Species		 Not uniquely human. Chimps show highest rates, gorillas lowest (higher if human-reared suggests experiential component) Chimps: 75%
Orangs: 60%
Gorillas: 30%
4 Cognitive Requirements
What It Shows		 Visual detection ‚Üí proprioceptive body schema mapping. Self-other differentiation foundation. Creates conceptual space for "others have minds too" Foundation for ToM

Requires: (1) touch marked area SPECIFICALLY (not random face exploration), (2) increased touching must exceed baseline + general increase, (3) mirror-inexperienced show progression: social responses ‚Üí exploration ‚Üí contingency testing ‚Üí recognition.

"What does contagious yawning research reveal about affective empathy in humans and great apes?"
# Study/Concept Key Finding Statistical Anchor

Concept		 "I feel the way you feel" ‚Äî emotional resonance. Mirror neurons for emotion. You smile ‚Üí I smile ‚Üí I feel happy ‚Üí I infer you feel happy. DON'T necessarily understand WHY Feeling vs Understanding

Out-group: +0.3/session
15√ó selectivity

LO1-C Cognitive Empathy: Altruistic Helping (Warneken)
"Describe the Warneken helping paradigm and explain what it reveals about cognitive empathy in humans and apes..."
# Component Details Statistical Anchor
1 Cognitive Empathy Defined
Concept		 Response is NOT just a mirror of other. Response demonstrates UNDERSTANDING that other's state is different from self. "I know what you need" Understanding ‚â† Resonance
2 Warneken Paradigm
Method		 Subjects retrieve out-of-reach objects for experimenters. 2√ó2: Reaching vs No-reaching √ó Reward vs No-reward. Test with unfamiliar individuals AND cross-species Controls for reward, familiarity
3 Results
Both Species		 Both 18-month-olds AND adult chimpanzees help at similar rates when adult reaches. Persists without reward, with unfamiliar individuals, cross-species 40-60% help rate
Reach: 45% vs No-reach: 8%
4 Altruism Calculation
Key Stat		 Altruistic specificity = [P(help|reach,no-reward) - P(help|no-reach,no-reward)] / P(help|reach,reward) = (0.45-0.08)/0.50 = 0.74 74% genuinely altruistic
5 Interpretation
Theory		 Altruistic helping = shared evolutionary foundation, not uniquely human morality. Helping is contingent on reading intention/need, NOT learned through reward Prosocial across species
‚ö†Ô∏è METHODOLOGICAL TRAP (LO2): Why is "reaching vs not-reaching" manipulation CRITICAL? Without it, high retrieval rates could reflect random object manipulation. The sharp drop (45% ‚Üí 8%) when experimenter doesn't reach confirms helping is INTENTION-DIRECTED, not random exploration.
LO1-D Goals & Intentions: Mummy-Ball / Baby-Ball Paradigm
"Explain the mummy-ball paradigm and what it reveals about infant understanding of goal-directed action..."
üìä Gergely et al. (1995); Csibra et al. (1999) ‚Äî Habituation Paradigm
HABITUATION: Small ball (baby) jumps OVER central barrier to reach large ball (mummy). Looking time decreases: 50s (trial 1) ‚Üí 12s (trial 6) = event encoded.
EXPERIMENTAL GROUP: Jump can be interpreted as INSTRUMENTAL ‚Äî necessary to reach mummy ball.
CONTROL GROUP: Barrier on SIDE (no obstacle) ‚Äî jump is IRRATIONAL from start.
TEST (barrier removed):
‚Ä¢ Path A (jumping continues): Motor pattern SAME but now IRRATIONAL (no obstacle)
‚Ä¢ Path B (rolling): Motor pattern NEW but RATIONAL (efficient path)
Age Group Result Interpretation
9-Month-Olds Look 42s at irrational jumping vs 18s at rational rolling.
Violation index = (42-12)/(18-12) = 30/6 = 5.0		 5√ó stronger surprise at IRRATIONALITY than NOVELTY. Encode actions TELEOLOGICALLY (means to ends)
6-Month-Olds No differential response between old/new actions Track GOALS but NOT efficiency yet. Rationality principle not yet online
Control Group No difference between continued jumping and rolling Neither action is rational/irrational when barrier was never instrumental
RATIONALITY PRINCIPLE: Expect agents to take efficient paths to goals given constraints
6mo: Goal tracking (WHERE is it going?) | 9mo: Efficiency expectation (WHY that path?)
‚ö†Ô∏è HABITUATION TRAP (LO2): Must distinguish surprise at PERCEPTUAL NOVELTY from surprise at VIOLATED EXPECTATIONS. Key controls: (1) Control condition with identical perceptual change but no rationality violation shows NO increase, (2) "Surprising" event is perceptually FAMILIAR (same motion), (3) Longer looking at OLD irrational than NEW rational.
LO1-E Rationality Without Mind-Reading: Chimp Foot-Pressing
"Explain how the chimp foot-pressing study demonstrates rationality understanding without mental state attribution..."
Condition Demonstration Chimp Response
A: Hands Occupied
(Constrained)		 Human presses button with FOOT while carrying bucket (hands not free) Chimp uses HANDS to press button
"Foot-use was necessary due to constraint ‚Üí I'll use hands (easier)"
B: Hands Free
(Unconstrained)		 Human presses button with FOOT while hands are free Chimp copies FOOT action
"Foot-use must be REQUIRED by button mechanism ‚Üí I'll use foot"

‚ö° KEY INSIGHT: Teleological ‚â† Mental State Reasoning

Selective copying based on constraint presence demonstrates organisms can judge action EFFICIENCY relative to situational limitations WITHOUT attributing mental states like beliefs/desires. Teleological reasoning operates BELOW theory of mind ‚Äî "in order to" relationships without hidden mental content.
LO1/LO3 The Developmental Paradox: Others Before Self
"Why is it paradoxical that intention understanding develops before self-recognition?"
Age Capacity Evidence Implication
6mo Goal tracking (not efficiency) Mummy-ball: no differential looking Represent WHERE agent is going
9mo Teleological reasoning Mummy-ball: 5√ó longer looking at irrational Expect efficiency given constraints
15-18mo Altruistic helping Warneken: 40-60% retrieve without reward Understand other's need differs from own state
24mo Mirror self-recognition Rouge test: 50% pass Recognize self as distinct entity
36mo Reliable self-recognition Rouge test: 90% pass Consolidated self-concept

‚ö° THE PARADOX EXPLAINED

Intuitive "building-block" models predict: Self-awareness ‚Üí Other-awareness. But evidence shows REVERSE: Understanding others' goals (6-9mo) ‚Üí Recognizing self (24mo). This 12-18 month window where infants understand others' intentions BETTER than their own identity contradicts sequential stage models. Suggests PARALLEL developmental pathways, not hierarchical stages.

LO4 Implications for ASD & Child Development
"How does social cognition research inform understanding of autism spectrum disorder?"
Finding Implication for ASD Clinical Relevance
Implicit vs Explicit ToM HFASD: Explicit ToM intact (can reason about beliefs), implicit ToM impaired (don't spontaneously track others' perspectives) Absent SPONTANEOUS ToM differs from absent CAPACITY. Can teach explicit compensation
Empathy Distinction ASD: Cognitive empathy IMPAIRED (don't understand), Affective empathy INTACT (do feel). OPPOSITE of CU traits (L10) Don't assume no empathy ‚Äî feel but don't understand
Attention Patterns ASD: Don't spontaneously orient to social stimuli (eyes, faces) ‚Äî similar to CU traits but different mechanism Explicit instruction to attend may compensate
Parallel Development Social cognition develops through multiple channels ‚Äî one pathway impaired doesn't mean all impaired Assess each capacity separately, target specific deficits

‚ö° CROSS-LECTURE LINK: L05 Social Cognition ‚Üí L10 CU Traits

ASD: Cognitive empathy IMPAIRED, Affective empathy INTACT ("feel but don't understand")
CU Traits: Cognitive empathy INTACT, Affective empathy IMPAIRED ("understand but don't feel")
Pattern REVERSAL is the key differentiator. Both show social cognition deficits but via opposite mechanisms.
