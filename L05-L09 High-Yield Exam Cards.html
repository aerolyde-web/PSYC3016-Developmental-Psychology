Theory of Mind: Two-Pathway Framework (L05-L07)

Theory of Mind #1
What is the Two-Pathway Hypothesis and why does it matter?
Click to reveal answer
Theory of Mind
Implicit ToM: Fast, automatic, unconscious. Emerges ~15 months. Measured via looking time (VoE). Shared with apes. Neural: mirror system.

Explicit ToM: Slow, deliberate, conscious. Emerges ~4-5 years. Measured via verbal prediction (Sally-Anne). Uniquely human. Neural: mentalizing network (mPFC, TPJ).

Key insight: Systems are INDEPENDENT, not developmental stages. The 3-year gap reflects PFC maturation, not regression.
Theory of Mind #2
What is the key evidence for two-pathway INDEPENDENCE? (Double dissociation)
Click to reveal answer
Theory of Mind
Double Dissociation Proof:

Great Apes: Pass implicit (VoE) / Fail explicit (verbal)
HFASD Adults: Fail implicit (no anticipatory gaze) / Pass explicit (learned rules)
Down Syndrome: Pass BOTH despite lower IQ than ASD

If one system, damage would impair both equally. Opposite pattern proves INDEPENDENCE. ToM is MODULAR, not "general smarts."

Theory of Mind #10
What is Tomasello's "shared intentionality" and why is it uniquely human?
Click to reveal answer
Theory of Mind
Tomasello - Shared Intentionality ("We-mode")

Components: Joint goals, mutual knowledge, collaborative roles, common ground

Apes lack this: They collaborate only when each action independently yields reward. No complementary roles toward shared goal.

Human 12mo: Point DECLARATIVELY to share information
Chimps: NEVER point to inform, only to request (imperative)

Foundation of language, morality, culture.

Moral Development #19
How does intention-outcome weighting develop?
Click to reveal answer
Moral Development
Piaget (1932) / Heyman & Gelman (1998)

Formula: J = α×Intention + β×Outcome

Kindergarten: 5:1 outcome-dominant
(β=0.7, α=0.3)

Adults: 4:1 intention-dominant
(α=0.8, β=0.2)

TRAP: "Kids ignore intentions" - FALSE! They use BOTH but overweight outcomes. Complete reversal with development.

Moral Development #38
What is the Harm Principle in domain theory?
Click to reveal answer
Moral Development
Harm Principle (Turiel, 1983):

Adding HARM to any behavior converts it from conventional to moral domain.

Example:
- Eating with hands = CONVENTIONAL (context-dependent, not serious)
- Eating with hands that burned someone = MORAL (authority-independent, serious)

"Harm is the switch" - presence of harm/victim shifts domain classification.

Also: Nudity = conventional; Nudity in Arctic = moral (harm from cold)

Moral Development #41
Darwin's Dual-Instinct model of guilt?
Click to reveal answer
Moral Development
Darwin's Dual-Instinct Model:

Social instinct (S): Constant, stable (e.g., S=5)
Appetite instinct A(t): Spikes at temptation, then fades
Formula: A(t) = 10×e^(-t/2)

Guilt emerges when: S > A
After transgression, appetite satisfied and fades, but social instinct persists → guilt!

Sentimentalist implication: Guilt is FEELING-based, not reasoning-based.
