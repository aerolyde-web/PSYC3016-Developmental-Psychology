Rouge Test Connection L08 Multiple studies 18mo-8yr Self-awareness assessment as moral prerequisite Self-awareness → moral agency → internalized standards 50% pass at 2yr Prerequisite for moral responsibility "Self-awareness at 18mo enables moral agency; internalized conscience by 8yr"
6-Stage Model L08 Kohlberg (1958-1981) Childhood to adulthood Structured interviews with moral dilemmas Pre-conventional → Conventional → Post-conventional reasoning Stage 3-4: Most adults
Stage 5: 10-15%
Stage 6: ~1%		 Cognitive-developmental approach "Kohlberg's 6 stages: punishment (1) → self-interest (2) → approval (3) → law (4) → social contract (5) → universal principles (6, ~1%)"
Care vs Justice L08 Gilligan (1982) Gender differences Critique of Kohlberg's male-only sample Females → Stage 3 (care); Males → Stage 4 (justice) Different orientations, not hierarchical Gender bias in moral reasoning theory "Gilligan (1982): Kohlberg's male sample created bias - care (Stage 3, female) vs justice (Stage 4, male) are different orientations"
Moral-Conventional Distinction L08 Turiel (1970s-80s) All ages Social Domain Theory framework Moral = harm-based, universal; Conventional = rule-based, context-dependent Authority-modifiable test Domain specificity in moral cognition "Turiel's domains: moral (harm, universal, unalterable) vs conventional (rules, context-bound, modifiable)"
M/C Distinction Study L08 Smetana (1981) 30-57mo Testing moral vs conventional understanding NO age differences - 30-month-olds distinguish moral from conventional Even 2.5yr olds show distinction Early moral competence "Smetana (1981): 30-month-olds distinguish moral (universal) from conventional (modifiable) - NO age differences"
Ecological Validity L08 Eisenberg (1980s) All ages Critique of hypothetical dilemmas Hypothetical ≠ real moral pressure Lab findings may not generalize Methodological critique "Eisenberg: hypothetical dilemmas cannot replicate real-world moral decision-making pressure"

Dual Instinct Model L09 Darwin (1871) Theoretical Descent of Man - moral emotions Social instincts (constant) vs Appetites (sudden); dissatisfaction = guilt S = 5 (constant), A(t) = 10×e^(-t/2) Evolutionary basis of guilt "Darwin: social instincts (constant, moderate) conflict with appetites (sudden, strong) creating guilt/remorse"
Violence Inhibition Mechanism L09 Blair (2005) Psychopathy groups Emotion recognition & moral behavior Distress cues → withdrawal → negative reinforcement Fear/sadness errors: 4.73 (high) vs 2.20 (low) Empathy-morality link "Blair's VIM: distress cues trigger withdrawal; impaired in psychopathy (4.73 vs 2.20 fear/sadness errors)"
Happy Victimizer L09 Nunner-Winkler & Sodian (1988) 4-8yr Emotion attribution to transgressors Age 4: thief feels happy; Age 8: thief feels guilty HV score: +1.35 (age 5) → -0.50 (age 8) Knowledge-emotion dissociation "Happy Victimizer: 4yr attribute happiness to successful thief despite knowing stealing is wrong"
Water Hose Study L09 Heyman & Gelman (1998) 5yr vs adults 2×2 factorial: Intention × Outcome Complete reversal: Kindergarten outcome-focus → Adult intention-focus Kindergarten 5:1 outcome
Adults 4:1 intention		 Developmental weighting shift "Heyman & Gelman: kindergarteners weight outcome:intention 5:1, adults weight intention:outcome 4:1"
Meta-Analysis L09 Malti & Krettenauer (2013) 42 studies, 8000+ participants Moral emotions → behavior correlations Stronger link to inhibiting antisocial than promoting prosocial Antisocial r = -0.35
Prosocial r = +0.20		 Asymmetric moral emotion effects "Malti & Krettenauer: moral emotions inhibit antisocial (r=-0.35) more than promote prosocial (r=+0.20)"
Personality Attribution L09 Developmental studies 4-8yr Behavior→Trait→Behavior chain development Age 5: rapid reset (3 days); Age 8: stable traits (45 days) Update rule changes: 0.9 current → 0.4 current Trait stability development "Personality attribution: Age 5 resets in 3 days, Age 8 takes 45 days (15× longer) - traits solidify"
Sentimentalist Philosophy L09 Hume (1700s) Theoretical Moral behavior from emotion/empathy Feeling > reasoning as moral source - Sentimentalist tradition foundation "Hume's sentimentalism: moral behavior emerges from emotion/empathy, not pure reasoning"

Common Exam Traps & Corrections
Common Error Correct Understanding Evidence
Kohlberg measures moral behavior NO: Measures reasoning about what "should" be done Same behavior can stem from different stages
Young children lack moral understanding NO: 30-month-olds distinguish moral from conventional Smetana (1981) - no age differences
Children ignore intentions NO: They use BOTH but overweight outcomes Heyman & Gelman - 5:1 ratio, not 0 intention
Happy Victimizer = psychopathy NO: Normal at age 4-5, concerning only if persists past 8 Developmental phenomenon, not pathology
Stage 6 is achievable by most NO: ~1% reach it; most adults remain Stage 3-4 Kohlberg's own data
Moral reasoning predicts behavior NO: Poor correlation; many factors intervene Personality, context, emotions all matter
Care vs Justice = hierarchy NO: Different orientations, not levels Gilligan (1982) - gender bias critique
VIM explains all antisocial behavior NO: Blair revised - psychopathic children CAN feel empathy Context-dependent empathy possible
Prosocial = opposite of antisocial NO: Distinct constructs with different correlates Malti & Krettenauer - asymmetric effects
