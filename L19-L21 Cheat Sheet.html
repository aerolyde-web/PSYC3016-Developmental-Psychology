1. RELATIONAL LEARNING: Objects → Relations (L19)

  • Natural Partitions Hypothesis: World = objects + relations. Objects perceptually cohesive, bounded, trackable; relations dynamic, infinite variability. Prediction: Object categories BEFORE relational categories. Universal: English, Japanese, Korean, Mandarin, Navajo—NEVER reversed (nouns precede prepositions/verbs).
  • Relational Shift (Career of Similarity): Early = features ("uncle = beardy guy"); Later = relations ("uncle = parent's brother"). "Island" = beaches+palms → land surrounded by water. Relations harder: no perceptual boundaries, constant change, infinite ways to conceptualize.
  • Cross-Mapped Test: Object vs relation conflict = strongest test. True relational understanding = choose relation despite different objects. Example: Toma = "2 same animals facing" → choose turtles facing (relational) over pig+fish (object match).
Why is relational learning harder than object learning? → Objects perceptually cohesive/bounded; relations have no perceptual boundaries, change constantly, require comparison to extract structure from variable instances.

3. LANGUAGE: Dual Mechanisms for Relational Learning (L19)

  • Mechanism 1 - Invitation to Compare: Common label signals hidden commonality → triggers comparison search. "This is toma, this is also toma" → Child: "Why both tomas? Let me compare."
  • Mechanism 2 - Reification: Provides stable symbolic representation for transient perceptual patterns. Spatial relations (in, on, between) are continuous gradations → language discretizes. Languages carve space differently (English "in" vs Korean "kkita" tight/"nehta" loose).

4. NUMBER: Core Knowledge → Linguistic Bootstrapping (L19)

Core Knowledge Gap: System 1 (subitizing 1-3 precise) + System 2 (analog magnitude approximate) ≠ Exact arbitrary quantities (e.g., exactly 47)
  • Subset-Knower Stages (2-4.5yo): Pre-knower (recite but no meaning) → One-knower → Two → Three → Four → CP-knower (successor principle). Learn 1,2,3,4 as individual vocab words before inductive leap.
  • Successor Principle: Count-position(n) ↔ Set-size(n). Further-by-one in count = greater-by-one in set. Language structure → conceptual structure (NOT just labeling).
  • Piraha: No number words → STUCK in core knowledge. Subitizing intact, analog intact, but NO exact arithmetic beyond 3. Proof language necessary for exact number.
Carey bootstrapping: Count list provides structural analog for numerical order. Children map further-by-one (linguistic) to greater-by-one (numerical). Piraha without number words = stuck in core knowledge state.

5. PLAY: Implicit Competence vs Explicit Mastery (L20)

DomainPlay Reveals (Implicit)School Requires (Explicit)
ScientificHypothesis generation, sensitivity to confoundsCOV strategy, systematic testing, domain knowledge
LogicalCounterfactuals in pretense, deduction from premisesFormal syllogisms, validity evaluation
MathematicalClassification, patterning, magnitude comparisonVerbalized rules, number line, fractions
  • Gopnik Blicket: 4yo with unexpected toy cycles through 5 hypotheses in 2 minutes—surprise-triggered inquiry, hypothesis generation, systematic testing, theory revision.
  • Schultz & Bonawitz (2007): Jack-in-box. Confounded: 65% choose old toy (resolve ambiguity). Unconfounded: 62% choose novel (causal structure complete). Play duration ∝ causal uncertainty (information-seeking, not hedonic).
  • Lucas et al (2014): Blicket Machines. Adults: 73% one-cause → 68% one-cause (no transfer!). 4yo: 65% one-cause → 62% two-cause (transfer abstract structure!). Children MORE flexible when priors misleading.
  • COV Failure (Klahr): Children change ALL variables. Not intelligence failure—lack abstract meta-strategy + domain knowledge. Joseph & Patel (1990): Even scientists fail when theory doesn't flag confounds.
  • Schalk et al (Switzerland): Inquiry-based physics education—COV + domain concepts taught together. Success requires BOTH meta-strategy AND domain knowledge (what's worth controlling).
  • Historical Decline: 1981-1997 = 25% decline in free-play time. Gray et al (2023): correlated with rising psychopathology diagnoses.
Play reveals implicit competence (hypothesis testing, causal sensitivity) but NOT explicit mastery. COV requires explicit instruction + domain vocabulary + reflective prompting.

7. MATH PLAY & SES GAP: Language-Mediated Formalization (L20)

  • Seo & Ginsburg (2004): 4yo free play = classification, patterning, symmetry construction, magnitude comparison. SES: NO difference in spontaneous math play behaviors.
  • SES Gap Mechanism: Same implicit competence, different VERBALIZATION. High-SES: more reflective conversations ("Why sort that way?") → formalize implicit knowledge → portable concepts. Low-SES: knowledge remains procedural, context-bound.
  • 1/3 lb Burger Failure: Adults believed 1/4 > 1/3 (larger denominator = larger). Implicit magnitude sense ≠ explicit fraction understanding. Symmetry paradox: 4yo build symmetrical structures but fail algebraic definition.
Equity Implication: Play-based learning WITHOUT structured reflection may maintain SES gaps. Laissez-faire approach leaves children without reflective home dialogue unable to verbalize/generalize.

9. EXECUTIVE FUNCTION: Three Components (L21)

ComponentDefinitionGold Standard Task
Response InhibitionSuppress prepotent responses; delay gratificationStroop, Day-Night, Marshmallow
Cognitive FlexibilitySwitch between mental sets; reconfigure S-R mappingsDCCS (Dimensional Change Card Sort)
Working MemoryMaintain & manipulate in parallel; relational complexityHalford tasks, n-back
  • Developmental Timeline: 2-3yo poor inhibition, tantrums; 3-5yo DRAMATIC improvement (Day-Night: 70-90% errors → 10-20%); 5-6yo DCCS success; 7-11yo largest PFC growth; mid-20s peak; 30s+ slow decline (knowledge compensates).
  • PFC Maturation: Protracted development (birth → mid-20s). Peak growth 7-11yo. PFC-amygdala connectivity = emotion regulation; weaker = depression risk.

10. DCCS PARADOX: Know Rule ≠ Execute Rule (L21)

Phase3yo Performance5yo Performance
Pre-Switch (Shape Game)100% accuracy100%
Rule Check (verbal)100% correct pointing100%
Post-Switch (Color Game)0% (perseverates on shape)85-95%
  • NOT failure of: Attention (succeeds pre-switch), Memory (states rule correctly), Comprehension (verbal knowledge intact), Perception (sees both dimensions).
  • ACTUAL Failure: Cognitive flexibility—cannot reconfigure established S-R mapping. Goal representation too weak to override old habit. Immature PFC cannot disconnect behavior from first-learned mapping.
  • Halford/Andrews view: Really WM failure. Must bind color + shape simultaneously (binary relational complexity). Under-5s fail binary relations (DCCS = 2D binding problem).
DCCS paradox: 3yo states rule perfectly (100% verbal) but behavior locked to first mapping (0% post-switch). NOT comprehension failure—cognitive flexibility/WM limit. PFC too immature to reconfigure.

  • Carlson & Moses: EF predicts False Belief r = 0.66. Inhibition required to suppress own knowledge when attributing mental states. EF = key bottleneck for ToM.
  • Resolution: BOTH factors matter. EF improvements unlock latent competencies AND conceptual development occurs. Continuous development (not discrete Piagetian stages).
  • Lifespan Trajectory: Childhood EF enables knowledge acquisition → knowledge compensates for later EF decline. Need early EF to BUILD compensatory knowledge base. Why elderly ≠ 4yo: crystallized intelligence intact, decline only in NOVEL tasks.
  • Adolescent Paradox: Intelligence > impulse control (PFC-amygdala still maturing). Risk-taking peaks → drops post-25 (car insurance drops—behavioral economics evidence).
Competence vs Performance: Infants show ToM via looking time but fail explicit tasks until 5yo (inhibition deficit). Carlson & Moses r=0.66: EF predicts FB. Both EF + concepts develop continuously.

13. SES, STRESS & PFC: Risk Pathways (L21)

  • Hackman & Farah (2009): Low SES → reduced PFC volume + weaker connectivity. Stress hormones (cortisol) interfere with synapse formation. Effect SPECIFIC to PFC (not global brain impairment).
  • Three Mechanisms: (1) Chronic stress → impaired PFC growth; (2) Acute stress → WM interference (worry consumes capacity = test anxiety); (3) Less stimulation → reduced enrichment.
  • Beavers, Schneider, Pacheco: Depression risk linked to weaker PFC-amygdala white matter (emotion regulation pathway).
  • Moffitt et al (longitudinal): Self-control in middle childhood → criminality, health, wealth in adulthood. More robust than Marshmallow test (original claims overstated).
  • Achievement Gap Pathway: SES → EF + language deficits → school outcomes. EF + language = primary mediators.

14. TOOLS OF THE MIND: Vygotskian EF Intervention (L21)

  • Theoretical Basis: Vygotsky—language as cultural tool for self-regulation. External speech → private speech → internal speech (internalization). Scaffolds → internalized turn-taking.
  • Diamond et al (2007): Hearts & Flowers task. Control: 65%(congruent), 55%(incongruent), 50%(mixed). Tools: 70%, 68%, 72%. BIGGEST effect on most demanding conditions (mixed/switching).

Tools of Mind: Vygotskian scaffolding (ear/lips cards → internalized self-regulation). Diamond 2007: Biggest effects on most demanding tasks (mixed = 50%→72%). Language becomes self-control tool.
