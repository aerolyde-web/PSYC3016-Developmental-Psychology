CORE MODEL The Two-Pathway Hypothesis: Implicit vs Explicit Theory of Mind

üü¢ IMPLICIT PATHWAY

  • Onset: 9 months (looking time), 15mo (watermelon)
  • Measure: Eye-gaze, anticipatory looking, looking time
  • Process: Automatic, bottom-up, reflexive
  • Speed: 150-300ms
  • Neural: Temporo-parietal junction (TPJ)
  • Evolution: Phylogenetically ANCIENT (shared with apes)
  • Consciousness: Without awareness
  • Training: Cannot be consciously controlled

üîµ EXPLICIT PATHWAY

  • Onset: 4 years (Sally-Anne verbal)
  • Measure: Verbal report, deliberate prediction
  • Process: Controlled, top-down, effortful
  • Speed: 2000-3000ms
  • Neural: Prefrontal cortex (PFC)
  • Evolution: HUMAN-UNIQUE innovation
  • Consciousness: Conscious reasoning
  • Training: CAN be taught (compensatory learning)
DOUBLE DISSOCIATION PROOF:
Apes: Implicit ‚úì | Explicit ‚úó (never develops)
HFASD: Implicit ‚úó | Explicit ‚úì (learned by 10-14yr)
‚Üí Two SEPARATE systems, not one system with performance limits
LO1-A Belief-Desire Psychology: The Foundation of ToM
"Explain the transition from desire psychology to belief-desire psychology..."
# Concept/Study Key Finding Statistical Anchor
1 Core Claim
Wellman 1990		 Behavior guided by DESIRES (what we want) √ó BELIEFS (what we think is true). Actions = Intentions balancing desires and beliefs. Can't understand humans without beliefs. Action = Desire √ó Belief
2 Desire Psychology
Age 2-3		 Children understand DESIRES as subjective ("You want apple, I want banana"). But don't separate beliefs from reality. Only predict based on wants. 3yr = "desire psychologist"
3 Belief-Desire Psychology
Age 4+		 Children understand actions require BOTH desire AND belief. Can hold "true state" and "represented state" separately. Pass false belief tasks. 4yr = "belief-desire psychologist"
4 Wellman & Liu (2004)
Scale Study		 Hierarchical ToM scale showing desires understood BEFORE beliefs. Predictable sequence across children. Desires ‚Üí Beliefs (order invariant)
BEHAVIOR PREDICTION FORMULA:
Person A: Desire(apple) √ó Belief(fridge) = Action(go to fridge)
Person B: Desire(apple) √ó Belief(cupboard) = Action(go to cupboard)
‚Üí Identical desires + different beliefs = DIFFERENT actions
LO1-B False Belief Understanding: Cognitive Architecture (Sally-Anne)
"Explain the cognitive demands of the Sally-Anne false belief task..."
Phase Protagonist (Sally) Subject (Child) Cognitive Load
T1: Shared Knowledge Sees apple in basket Sees apple in basket 1 representation: [basket = apple]
T2: Protagonist Absent ‚Äî (gone) Sees apple ‚Üí banana switch Update: [basket = banana]
T3: Test (returns) Believes: apple in basket Knows: banana in basket DUAL: [reality = banana] + [Sally's belief = apple] + inhibit "banana" response
Age Pass Rate What's Missing Before Age 4?
3 years ~15% pass Reality bias dominates ‚Äî answer "banana" (reality, not false belief). Cannot maintain metarepresentation.
4 years ~85% pass Dual representation + inhibitory control online. Can answer "apple" (Sally's belief).

‚ö° WHY AGE 4 SPECIFICALLY?

1. Prefrontal cortex maturation (executive control)
2. Working memory capacity for dual representations
3. Language development ("thinks that" structures)
4. Inhibitory control to suppress reality bias (r = 0.66 with FB success, Carlson & Moses 2001)
LO1-C Implicit False Belief: Evidence for Early Automatic ToM
"What evidence supports implicit false belief understanding before age 4?"
# Study Method & Finding Statistical Anchor
1 Onishi & Baillargeon (2005)
Watermelon Task		 15-month-olds. Object transfer while actor absent. Looking time to belief-consistent vs belief-inconsistent search. Longer looking when agent searches where object IS (reality) vs where agent BELIEVES it to be. FB-consistent: 11.5s
FB-violation: 20.0s
2 Southgate et al. (2007)
Anticipatory Looking		 25-month-olds. Eye-tracking where agent will search. Correct anticipatory looking 1-2 years BEFORE verbal success. Eyes know before mouth can say
3 Kov√°cs et al. (2010)
Automatic Computation		 7-month-olds AND adults. RT to detect ball affected by agent's belief state. Faster RT when agent believes ball present ‚Äî automatic, without awareness. Both ages show automatic belief tracking
4 Krupenye et al. (2016)
Apes Implicit ToM		 Great apes (chimps, bonobos, orangutans). Eye-tracking during false belief scenarios. 70% anticipatory looking to false-belief location ‚Äî BUT 0% explicit behavioral success. 70% correct anticipatory gaze
0% explicit behavior

LO1-D Three Critical Distinctions (Exam Traps)
Distinction Simpler Form Complex Form
1. Ignorance vs Belief IGNORANCE: Track info access only ("doesn't know X"). Single representation. FALSE BELIEF: Dual representation ("thinks X when Y true"). Contradictory mental content + inhibition.
2. Coordination vs Collaboration COORDINATION: Parallel goals (chimps pick berries near each other). "I am doing X near others." COLLABORATION: Role differentiation + shared goal (shake tree, hold bucket). "We are doing X together."
3. Implicit vs Explicit ToM IMPLICIT: Automatic eye gaze (9mo, apes). 150-300ms. Cannot be consciously controlled. EXPLICIT: Conscious reasoning (4yr, verbal). 2000-3000ms. Can be learned/taught.
‚ö†Ô∏è COMMON ERROR: Equating "doesn't know X" with "believes not-X" ‚Äî these require DIFFERENT cognitive demands.
Ignorance = absence of knowledge (single representation). False belief = contradictory mental content (dual representation + inhibition).
Chimps succeed at IGNORANCE tasks (42% hidden food) but fail FALSE BELIEF (33% = chance).
LO4 The ASD Paradox: Explicit Success Despite Implicit Failure
"Explain the paradox of ToM in high-functioning autism..."
# Study Key Finding Statistical Anchor
1 Baron-Cohen et al. (1985)
Original Study		 Autism (CA 11yr, VMA 5yr) vs Down (CA 10yr, VMA 5yr) vs Typical (4yr). Sally-Anne task. Initial "no ToM" conclusion. BUT 4 autistic kids DID pass ‚Äî sparked trajectory research. Autism: 20% pass
Down + Typical: ~85% pass
2 Scheeren et al. (2013)
Large-Scale HFASD		 n=194 high-functioning ASD vs TD (matched IQ). 2nd-order false belief, faux pas, sarcasm, display rules. NO significant differences in adolescence. DELAY, not deficit. n = 194
No group differences by age 10-14
3 Senju et al. (2009)
Implicit/Explicit Dissociation		 HFASD adults (IQ 115). Perfect explicit ToM (Strange Stories = 100%). But implicit ToM via eye-tracking = 50% (chance level). Same answer via DIFFERENT neural route. Explicit: 100% correct
Implicit: 50% (chance)

‚ö° THE PARADOX EXPLAINED: DEVELOPMENTAL TRAJECTORIES

Typical: Implicit (9mo) ‚Üí Explicit (4yr) [Both pathways intact]
ASD: No implicit ‚Üí Explicit (10-14yr via compensatory learning) [Top-down only]
Apes: Implicit (yes) ‚Üí Explicit (never) [Ancient system only]

Clinical Implication: High performance on structured ToM tasks doesn't indicate typical social processing. Real-world interaction relies on AUTOMATIC implicit ToM (150-300ms). Conscious reasoning (2000-3000ms) is too slow for fluid social interaction ‚Äî explains why high-functioning individuals still experience social challenges despite understanding ToM concepts intellectually.
LO1 Shared Intentionality: Tomasello's Framework (Human Uniqueness)
"What is shared intentionality and why is it considered uniquely human?"
Concept Details
Shared Intentionality
Tomasello et al. 2005		 Commitment to joint goals BEYOND individual benefit. "We-mode" representation vs "I-mode." Chimps lack "we are doing X together" ‚Äî only "I am doing X near others."
Hide-and-Seek Pointing
Example		 14-month infants infer hidden object from point (shared attentional frame). Chimps see "bucket, so what?" ‚Äî missing the shared understanding that "we are both attending to this together."
Role Reversal Test
Key Evidence		 Humans succeed at role reversal in collaborative tasks (I shake tree now, you hold bucket; then switch). Apes fail ‚Äî can't represent "our" shared goal, only individual goals.
Why It Matters
Human Culture		 Shared intentionality = foundation for human culture, language, and cooperation. Transforms individual cognition into collaborative frameworks. Explains cumulative culture.
TRANSFORMATION SEQUENCE (Tomasello):
Gaze following (tracking) + Mutual knowledge of shared attention = JOINT ATTENTION ("we are attending together")
Group activity (parallel goals) + We-mode representation = COLLABORATION (role differentiation + shared goal)
Social learning (passive) + Common ground framework = INSTRUCTED LEARNING (shared teaching frame)

LO1 Remaining Questions (Critically Consider)
Question Current Debate
Submentalizing (Heyes 2014) Are infant "mindreading" results actually ToM, or just novelty preference, associations, domain-general learning? Rich vs lean interpretation debate.
Two Systems or One? Are implicit and explicit ToM truly separate systems, or one system with performance factors masking early competence?
Chimp Failure: Load vs Limit? Do chimps fail explicit ToM due to working memory overload, or fundamental metarepresentational limit?
Neural Specialization How do TPJ (implicit) and PFC (explicit) pathways interact? Is TPJ truly automatic or can it be modulated?
