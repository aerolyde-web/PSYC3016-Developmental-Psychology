LO1 Theoretical Frameworks: Sentimentalism vs Rationalism
"Compare sentimentalist and rationalist approaches to moral development..."

üíó SENTIMENTALISM (Hume, Darwin, Blair)

Core Claim: Moral behavior emerges from EMOTION/EMPATHY, not pure reasoning

Key Quote: "Children are moral BECAUSE they are empathic"

Mechanism: Distress cues ‚Üí withdrawal response ‚Üí negative reinforcement

Challenge: "Just because we feel pity does not imply we will act on that feeling" (Aristotle)

üß† RATIONALISM (Kohlberg)

Core Claim: Moral behavior emerges from COGNITIVE REASONING about principles

Key Focus: What people think they SHOULD do in hypothetical dilemmas

Mechanism: Stage progression through increasing cognitive sophistication

Limitation: Reasoning ‚â† Behavior (same behavior, different stages)

KEY DISTINCTION: Sentimentalists privilege FEELING over reasoning
Rationalists privilege THINKING over emotion
TRUTH: Most moral behavior involves BOTH systems
LO1 Violence Inhibition Mechanism (VIM) ‚Äî Blair's Psychopathy Model
"Explain the Violence Inhibition Mechanism and its role in moral development..."
Component Mechanism Evidence
VIM Definition
Blair 1995		 Cognitive mechanism activated by NON-VERBAL DISTRESS cues (sad face, tears). When activated ‚Üí initiates WITHDRAWAL response, terminating aggression. Creates negative reinforcement loop. Camras (1977): Distress cues terminate aggression in 4-7yr-olds
Psychopath Deficit "Psychopaths are impaired in their ability to form associations between their behaviour and socially-relevant cues (aversive unconditioned stimuli)" Violence + aggression overcome by sympathy/empathy in non-psychopaths
Fear Recognition
Blair 2005		 Boys with psychopathic tendencies: MORE ERRORS recognizing fear vocalizations. No differences for other emotions. SELECTIVE impairment. 4.73 vs 2.20 errors (fear/sadness)
M/C Distinction High PSD children show SMALLER moral-conventional gap. Rate moral violations as LESS SERIOUS (not conventional as more serious). 1.23 vs 1.84 seriousness gap
VIM MECHANISM: Distress Cue (80% intensity) > Threshold (60%) ‚Üí Withdrawal ‚Üí ŒîR = -0.2 units
Over 10 episodes: Cumulative reinforcement = -2.0 units
Aggression probability: 0.5 √ó e^(-2.0) ‚âà 0.068 (86% reduction)
‚ö†Ô∏è EXAM TRAP: Blair himself REVISED VIM theory ‚Äî children with psychopathic traits CAN feel empathy under certain conditions. VIM doesn't explain ALL antisocial behavior! The deficit is more nuanced than simple absence of mechanism.
LO1 Darwin's Dual-Instinct Model (Descent of Man)
"Explain Darwin's sentimentalist model of moral emotions..."
Instinct Type Characteristics Moral Implications
SOCIAL INSTINCTS
(Sympathy, Goodwill)		 CONSTANT and MODERATE pressure toward group cohesion, affiliation Always present, "ever in some degree active in his mind"

LO4 Contemporary Relevance: Cancel Culture & Kindergarten Reasoning
"How is the intention-outcome balance relevant to current social behavior?"

‚ö° Cultural Link: Cancel Culture Mirrors Kindergarten Moral Reasoning

Kindergarten reasoning: Emphasis on OUTCOME (who was hurt) over INTENTION (what was meant)
Cancel culture pattern: Emphasis on OUTCOME (harm caused) over INTENTION (what was meant)

Interpretation: Not "new phenomenon" but developmental REGRESSION under social pressure. Under conditions of high emotional arousal and social conformity pressure, adults may revert to outcome-dominant moral reasoning patterns characteristic of early childhood.
Why Outcome-Dominance? Explanation
Theory of Mind Limits Young children's limited ToM makes OUTCOMES more reliable indicators than inferred intentions
Evolutionary Heuristic Avoid outcome-causing individuals regardless of intention = safety heuristic
Social Pressure Group dynamics may favor quick outcome-based judgments over nuanced intention analysis
