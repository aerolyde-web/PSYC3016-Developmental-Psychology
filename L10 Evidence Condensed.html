CU vs ASD: EMPATHY ARCHITECTURE
CU vs ASD Empathy Profile CU vs ASD youth CU: intact cognitive, impaired affective. ASD: impaired cognitive, intact affective. Both: impaired implicit, intact explicit "CU: know how you feel, don't care. ASD: don't know, but would care if I did"
Implicit/Explicit Architecture HF-ASD; CU youth CU: implicit affective OFF, explicit ON when cued. ASD: implicit ToM OFF, explicit ToM ON via effort. Both reach answers via slow explicit routes "Same architecture as ToM: implicit broken, explicit compensates"
HOT vs COLD CONDUCT DISORDER
Hot vs Cold CD
Frick & Morris (2004)		 Clinic CD samples Hot (~70%): reactive, high anxiety, h²≈0.30-0.40, PMT success >70%. Cold CU (~30%): proactive, flat, h²≈0.81-0.82, PMT <30%, male 5-10:1 "Hot = emotional, environmental, treatable. Cold = unemotional, genetic, resistant"
DIFFERENTIAL AMYGDALA MODEL
BLA Function
Blair (2007)		 CU traits BLA = stimulus-reinforcement learning. CU: reduced activation to fearful faces (~0.8 vs ~0.2 = 75% reduction); impaired aversive conditioning "Value calculator broken" – underactive BLA → weak learning from distress
CeA Function Anxiety / hot CD CeA = fear output (autonomic + behavioural). Intact in CU → normal unconditioned fear. Hyperactive in hot CD → high anxiety, startle "Alarm stuck ON" (hot CD) vs "alarm works, value calculator broken" (CU)
Differential Model
Integrative account		 CU vs hot CD CU: BLA under + CeA normal. Hot CD: BLA normal + CeA over. Explains implicit deficit + explicit intact pattern "Amygdala ≠ one blob" – splitting BLA/CeA unifies contradictions
KEY EMPIRICAL FINDINGS
Fear Recognition
Dadds et al. (2006)		 CU vs TD children Free view: 45% accuracy, 30% eye fixations. "Look at eyes": 78% accuracy, 80% fixations. 73% improvement "45%→78% with 'look at eyes'" – attention deficit, not emotion blindness
Conditioned Fear
Newman et al. (2010)		 CU vs typical Fear-potentiated startle: typical 40-60%, CU 10-20% (50-67% reduction). Unconditioned startle normal. Attention cue restores to 35-50% "Not fearless – don't LEARN from fear" unless attention forced
Selective Fear Blindness
Marsh & Blair (2008)		 Meta-analysis Fear recognition: d≈−0.52. No deficit for happiness, anger, surprise. Fear-specific "Blind to fear, not to faces" – fits VIM + BLA specialization
Iowa Gambling Task
Blair; Mitchell		 CU adolescents/adults Trial ~50: typical ~70% advantageous decks; CU ~45%. CU can verbally state which decks bad yet keep choosing "Know it's bad, keep choosing it" – explicit intact, implicit broken
Passive Avoidance
Blair; Newman		 CU adolescents/adults Stop pressing after: typical 3-5 punishments, CU 8-12 (2-3× slower). Acquisition intact "Learn rewards fine, don't stop when they turn to losses"
Distress Cue Avoidance
Dadds et al. (2016)		 CU vs controls CU kids actively avoid distress faces; effect d≈0.60-0.80 "To learn empathy you must look at pain – these kids look away"
PRIMARY vs SECONDARY PSYCHOPATHY
Primary (Constitutional)
Frick & Blair		 CU+ children Anxiety T<40, onset <3yr, h²≈0.81-0.82, male 5-10:1, PTSD <5%, BLA underactive from birth "Primary = born cold" – constitutional neurodevelopmental
Secondary (Trauma)
Frick & Blair		 CU-like with trauma Anxiety T>60, trauma precipitant, male ≈2:1, PTSD >40%, h²≈0.30-0.40. Trauma CBT: 60-70% response "Secondary = turned cold" – treatable, anxiety is leverage
Heritability × PMT
Viding et al. (2005)		 Twins; CD ± CU Low CU: h²≈0.30, PMT 60%. High CU: h²≈0.81, PMT 20%. 51-point h² gap, 3× treatment diff "Same diagnosis, 51-point h² gap, 3× treatment difference"
DEVELOPMENTAL & CROSS-LECTURE LINKS
Developmental Snowball
Moffitt (1993); Frick (2016)		 Early CU → adolescence CU → peer rejection → deviant peers → school fail → crime. Reputation reset: 3 days at 5yr → 45 days at 8yr (15× stickier) "Intervene before Year 3 or you're fighting concrete"
L10 ↔ L05-07 (ToM) Architecture parallel HF-ASD: implicit ToM–/explicit ToM+. CU: implicit empathy–/explicit empathy+. Both use effortful PFC to compensate "Same architecture, different domain" – dual-pathway applies to empathy
L10 ↔ L09 (VIM) M/C tasks; CU Blair PSD data: M/C gap 1.23 vs 1.84. Without VIM, harm stays "just a rule" "No VIM, no morality" – CU treat hitting as conventional
