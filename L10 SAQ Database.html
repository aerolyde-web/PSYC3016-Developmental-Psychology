‚ö° KEY INSIGHT: Theoretical Evolution

Each theory captures partial truth. The solution emerges from the Differential Amygdala Activation Model ‚Äî distinguishing automatic (BLA) from explicit (CeA) processing explains ALL contradictory findings.
LO1-B Differential Amygdala Activation Model (The Solution)
"Explain the differential amygdala activation model and how it resolves contradictions in the CU traits literature..."
Basolateral Amygdala (BLA) UNDERACTIVE
  • Automatic attention allocation
  • Encodes specific features (is it $1 or $5?)
  • Fast prediction error response
  • Evolutionarily NEW (mammalian)
  • Quick contingency updating
Central Amygdala (CeA) INTACT
  • Explicit processing
  • Encodes general valence (good/bad)
  • Physiological fear response
  • Phylogenetically ANCIENT (lizard brain)
  • Slow updating, maintains emotional tone

LO4 Implications for Parenting & Clinical Treatment
"How does research on CU traits inform clinical intervention and parenting approaches?"
# Implication Explanation ‚Üí SAQ Function
1 Why Standard Interventions Fail Traditional behavioral interventions ASSUME automatic social learning is intact. They present social cues expecting children to naturally attend and learn. CU children's BLA underactivation means they DON'T automatically attend Explains treatment failure
2 Why Attention Interventions Work Explicitly directing attention ("look at the eyes") bypasses damaged automatic system (BLA) and engages INTACT explicit processing (CeA). Fear recognition normalizes with instruction Evidence-based solution
3 Make Social Learning Explicit Since explicit systems remain functional, interventions should teach compensatory strategies ‚Äî making social learning EXPLICIT rather than assuming implicit acquisition Treatment principle
4 Early Intervention Critical Developmental snowball: every month of impaired automatic social attention COMPOUNDS deficits. Earlier intervention = less accumulated deficit. Critical window: before age 7, earliest possible age 3 Timing rationale
5 Differentiate Hot from Cold Hot children (70%): respond well to parent management training ‚Äî address emotional dysregulation. Cold children (30%): need specialized CU-focused intervention ‚Äî explicit social teaching Treatment matching
6 Parenting Implications Standard punishment ineffective for CU children (only insensitive when rewards present). Need to: (1) explicitly teach emotion recognition, (2) direct attention to social cues, (3) make consequences explicit not implicit Parent guidance
Intervention Formula: Bypass BLA (damaged) ‚Üí Engage CeA (intact) ‚Üí Make implicit EXPLICIT ‚Üí Direct attention to social cues

‚ö° THE DEVELOPMENTAL IMPERATIVE

CU traits are SUBTLE. The behaviors are NOT. The difference is TIME. A 0.6 difference in automatic BLA activation cascades into massive behavioral differences over years of missed social learning. But explicit instruction recovers ~80% of normal function ‚Äî if we intervene early.
