Q1
According to Chomsky's Universal Grammar (UG) theory, what is the primary claim about language acquisition?
A) Children learn language purely through statistical patterns in input
B) Children have innate knowledge of abstract syntactic structures that constrain learning
C) Language learning is identical to learning any other cognitive skill
D) All languages have identical surface structures
üí° Explanation:
Universal Grammar (UG) claims children are born with innate knowledge of universal principles that govern all human languages. These include abstract syntactic categories (noun phrases, verb phrases) and structural principles (e.g., hierarchical phrase structure, structural dependence). Languages vary in parameters (word order, etc.), but the deep structural principles are universal and innate. This explains how children acquire complex syntax rapidly from limited input‚Äîthe "poverty of stimulus" argument.
üîë Core Claim: "People obtain knowledge of the structure of language for which no evidence is available in the data to which they are exposed as children." ‚ÄîChomsky

üí° Explanation:

Q9

C) Rules that children learn through trial and error

üí° Explanation:
Structural dependence means syntactic operations care about phrase structure, not just word position. Example in question formation:
  • Declarative: "The boy is crazy" ‚Üí "Is the boy crazy?"
  • Declarative: "The boy who is smoking is crazy"
  • Question: "Is the boy who is smoking crazy?" ‚úì
  • NOT: "*Is the boy who smoking is crazy?" ‚úó
You move the "is" that's part of the main verb phrase (closest to S node), NOT the first "is" you encounter. This requires understanding hierarchical structure.
üéØ Key Insight: Can't use simple linear rule like "move first verb." Must understand which verb is structurally part of main clause.
