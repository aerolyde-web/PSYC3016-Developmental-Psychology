1. PREREQUISITES FOR MORAL AGENCY & KOHLBERG'S STAGES

  • Self-Awareness (18-24mo): Rouge test 50% pass at 2yr. Must recognize "I" before "I did wrong." Amsterdam (1972). Self-conscious emotions: 2yr pride/shame WITH audience → 4-6yr differentiate from basic → 8yr self-appraisal WITHOUT audience = internalized conscience.
  • Theory of Mind (4yr): False belief. Pre-ToM = OUTCOME only; post-ToM = incorporate intention. ToM required for Stage 3+ (must understand what OTHERS think/expect). Stage 1-2 = egocentric, no ToM needed.
  • Kohlberg's 6 Stages: Measures REASONING structure, NOT decisions. Pre-conventional (1-2): Punishment/self-interest. Conventional (3-4): Social approval/law & order. Post-conventional (5-6): Social contract/universal principles.
  • Same Behavior, Different Stages: Man punches bus harasser → Stage 2 ("impress woman"), Stage 3 ("meet expectations"), Stage 6 ("protect vulnerable"). Cannot infer reasoning from behavior!
  • Most adults = Stage 3-4. Only 10-15% reach Stage 5; ~1% Stage 6 (dropped from manual). 20-year longitudinal: Invariant sequence, no stage-skipping.
Cascade: Self(18mo)→ToM(4yr)→Intent→Agency. Self-conscious emotions: 2yr WITH audience → 8yr WITHOUT = internalized. Kohlberg = WHY not what. Same behavior = different stages!

3. MORAL-CONVENTIONAL DISTINCTION (Turiel/Smetana)

  • Smetana (1981): Children as young as 30 months (2.5yr) distinguish moral from conventional. NO age differences—challenges Kohlberg's late-emerging stages! "Toddlers know the difference."
  • Harm Principle: Adding harm CONVERTS convention → moral. "Harm is the switch." How children LEARN: Adults react with intense distress to hitting vs mild correction for dress code → different response teaches domain.
4 tests: Authority-indep? Rule-indep? Generalizable? Serious? Moral=YES to all. Smetana: 30mo already distinguish! Harm shifts convention→moral domain.

4. VIM & SENTIMENTALIST TRADITION (Blair)

  • Violence Inhibition Mechanism (Blair, 1995): Innate system: distress cues → autonomic arousal → aggression inhibition. Creates emotional foundation for M/C distinction. Two deficits: (1) Recognition: Can't identify fear/sadness. (2) Response: Recognize but don't feel aversive.
  • Sentimentalist Tradition (Hume, Darwin, Blair): Moral behavior from EMOTION/empathy, not pure reasoning. Emotion is NECESSARY. "Must FEEL to judge minds."
  • Blair (1997) Psychopathy Evidence: VIM impaired → Fear/sadness recognition errors: 4.73 vs 2.20. M/C seriousness diff: 1.23 (high PSD) vs 1.84 (low PSD). Say hitting "OK if no rule"—treating moral as conventional.
  • VIM Revision: Blair later updated—children with psychopathic traits CAN feel empathy under certain conditions (explicit instruction, close relationships). Not complete absence but reduced automatic response.
  • Darwin's Dual-Instinct: Social instincts (constant, moderate) vs Appetites (sudden, strong). Transgression = appetite wins → when social returns = guilt/remorse. "Talk is cheap"—reasoning alone predicts poorly.
VIM: Distress→aversion→inhibition. TWO deficits: recognition OR response. Psychopathy: 4.73 vs 2.20 errors, treat moral as conventional. VIM revised: not absent, reduced automatic.

5. HAPPY VICTIMIZER: Moral Knowledge vs Emotional Binding

QuestionAge 4-5 ResponseAge 8+ Response
Is stealing wrong?YES (know the rule)YES (know the rule)
How does thief feel?HAPPY ("got what wanted")BAD/GUILTY ("did wrong")
  • Nunner-Winkler & Sodian (1988): Children KNOW rules but predict positive emotion for transgressors. Age 4-5: ~70% "happy". Age 8+: ~80% "guilty". "Head knows, heart doesn't."
  • Transition Markers: 4yr = pure HV (happy only). 5-6yr = priming-sensitive (question order matters). 7yr = mixed emotions ("happy AND sad"). 8yr = moral emotion dominant.
  • HV Scoring: +2 (happy) → -2 (guilt). Age 5 mean = +1.35; Age 8 mean = -0.50. Shift = 1.85 units over 3yr ≈ 0.62/yr. Emotional binding = knowledge becomes personally aversive.
  • Priming Effects: "Is stealing wrong?" FIRST → ↑ guilt. "Bob really wanted lollies" FIRST → ↑ happy. Order matters at 5-7, not at 4 (too young) or 8 (already bound).
  • NOT psychopathy at age 4-5! Normal stage. Concerning only if persists past 8 WITH other signs (no empathy in other contexts, callous-unemotional traits).
HV paradigm: Know it's wrong but predict thief feels happy (70% at 4-5yr → 20% at 8yr). Moral knowledge precedes emotional binding. Priming shifts responses. NOT pathology at young ages!

6. INTENTION vs OUTCOME WEIGHTING DEVELOPMENT

Judgment = α × Intention + β × Outcome | Age 5: α≈0.3, β≈0.7 (5:1) | Age 8: α≈0.6, β≈0.4 | Adults: α≈0.8, β≈0.2 (4:1 reversed)
ConditionIntentOutcomeKindyAdultDifference
P+O+GoodGood8/107/10-1 (similar)
P+O-GoodBad3/106/10+3 (intent saves)

  • Piaget (1932) Moral Realism: 15 cups accidentally > 1 cup intentionally = "more naughty." Outcome focus before intention focus.
  • Heyman & Gelman (1998): P+O- shows biggest developmental change: Good intent + bad outcome. Kindy = bad (3/10); Adults = OK (6/10). Adults forgive accidents.
  • SHIFT in weights, not replacement. Children DO understand intentions—just overweight outcomes. Outcomes never become irrelevant (even adults = 2/10 for P-O-).
  • ToM Dependency: Killen et al. (2011): False belief → earlier intention weighting. ToM training accelerates shift. "Must read minds to weight minds."
Heyman 2×2: P+O- shows biggest dev change (3→6). Kindy 5:1 outcome, Adults 4:1 intent. SHIFT not replacement—outcomes still matter. ToM required for intention weighting.

7. PERSONALITY ATTRIBUTION & MALTI META-ANALYSIS

AgeAttribution PatternUpdate Rule (Persistence)
4-5 yearsSEPARATE: Behavior→Trait OR Trait→Behavior (not chained). "Selfish today, fresh start tomorrow."New = 0.1×Prev + 0.9×Current (~3 days)

  • Malti & Krettenauer (2013): 42 studies, n>8,000. Guilt → antisocial: r = -.39 (STRONG). Guilt → prosocial: r = +.26 (moderate). Asymmetry = guilt is BRAKE not engine.
  • Asymmetry explained: "Guilt stops bad more than starts good." Guilt = inhibition ("don't hurt"). Prosocial requires extra motivation (empathic concern, pride) beyond guilt avoidance.
  • Anticipatory Guilt: "I'd feel bad, so I won't" = emotional brakes. HV responses predict actual behavior. Age 5: guilt attributor = -40% transgression vs non-attributor.
  • Clinical Window: Intervene BEFORE 7-8 when peer attributions solidify (3d → 45d = 15× longer). After Grade 2, "aggressive" label = years of self-fulfilling prophecy.
Malti: r=-.39 (antisocial) vs r=+.26 (prosocial). Guilt=BRAKE. Attribution: 3d (age 5) → 45d (age 8) = 15× longer. Intervene BEFORE Grade 2 when labels stick!

8. DEVELOPMENT PARADOX: Understanding ≠ Behavior

  • Development Paradox: 3yr knows hitting is wrong but still hits sibling. Moral understanding emerges EARLY (30mo); behavioral control requires years of development.
  • Why the gap? (1) Immature impulse control—can't stop action even when knowing wrong. (2) Emotional regulation—anger overrides knowledge. (3) Internalization process: External understanding → internal behavioral control is gradual. (4) Situational factors: Group pressure, social anxiety override reasoning.
  • Three-Level Assessment: (1) Rule Knowledge: "Is stealing wrong?" (by 2.5yr). (2) Emotional Attribution: "How does thief feel?" (develops 4→8). (3) Personal Binding: How child feels when THEY transgress (observe actual behavior).
  • Common Exam Errors: (1) M/C (Turiel: harm-based) ≠ Care/Justice (Gilligan: gender). (2) HV at 4-5 ≠ psychopathy (normal!). (3) Kids DO understand intent—just overweight outcome. (4) Stage 6 is rare/theoretical. (5) Recognition ≠ Response deficit in VIM.
Paradox: 30mo know M/C but still violate. Gap = impulse control + emotional reg + internalization. 3 levels: Rule knowledge (2.5yr) → Emotional attribution (4-8yr) → Personal binding (observe behavior).
