PRACTICE Q1: Gene √ó Environment Interaction (Likely exam topic)
"Explain how the differential susceptibility hypothesis challenges the traditional diathesis-stress model, using the Fast Track study as your primary evidence." (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS The differential susceptibility hypothesis fundamentally reframes genetic "vulnerability" as environmental sensitivity that operates "for better AND for worse," contrasting with diathesis-stress which predicts vulnerability only "for worse."

EVIDENCE The Fast Track RCT tracked high-risk first graders to age 25, examining NR3C1 glucocorticoid receptor gene variants. STATS A-allele carriers (orchids) showed 75% externalizing disorders in control conditions but only 18% with intervention‚Äîa 57-point improvement. GG homozygotes (dandelions) showed 50%‚Üí50% regardless of condition.

MECHANISM The crossover interaction pattern is the signature: the same genetic variant that produces worst outcomes in adversity produces best outcomes in enrichment. Orchids wither in harsh conditions but bloom spectacularly in optimal environments; dandelions grow anywhere but never excel.

VERDICT This pattern CONTRADICTS diathesis-stress, which predicts A-carriers would show poor outcomes in adversity but merely average outcomes in enrichment (no crossover). Differential susceptibility explains why analyzing main effects alone shows modest intervention effects‚Äîthe genotype √ó treatment interaction is obscured when subgroups are collapsed. The theoretical implication is that "vulnerability genes" should be reconceptualized as "plasticity genes" conferring heightened environmental responsiveness.

‚ö†Ô∏è EXAM TRAP: Don't confuse mediation (HOW: sequential pathway) with moderation (WHEN/FOR WHOM: interaction effect). G√óE is MODERATION‚Äîgenotype changes the STRENGTH of environment's effect.
PRACTICE Q2: Implicit vs Explicit Theory of Mind (High probability)
"The two-pathway hypothesis proposes that implicit and explicit Theory of Mind represent dissociable systems. Evaluate this claim using evidence from both typical development and autism spectrum disorder." (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS The two-pathway hypothesis posits that ToM operates via two dissociable systems: an implicit pathway (automatic, eye-gaze based, phylogenetically ancient) and an explicit pathway (deliberative, verbally mediated, human-unique). Double dissociation evidence confirms pathway independence.

EVIDENCE 1 - Typical Development: Onishi & Baillargeon (2005) demonstrated implicit false belief at 15 months via looking time (mean 39.3s vs 29.1s for belief-inconsistent reaches). Yet children fail explicit Sally-Anne tasks until age 4 (~15% at 3yo ‚Üí 85% at 4yo). This 2.5-year gap suggests different developmental trajectories.

EVIDENCE 2 - ASD: Senju et al. (2009) tested HFASD adults (IQ 115) on both pathways. STATS Explicit: 100% correct on Strange Stories. Implicit: 50% (chance level) anticipatory looking. This is the inverse pattern to apes (Krupenye 2016: 70% implicit, 0% explicit).

MECHANISM The double dissociation proves pathway independence: ASD shows explicit+/implicit-, apes show implicit+/explicit-. HFASD individuals "hack" explicit ToM via learned rules (prefrontal compensation, ~2000-3000ms reasoning) but cannot access automatic TPJ processing (~150-300ms). This explains the paradox of high-functioning individuals passing lab tasks but struggling with real-time social interaction.

VERDICT These findings SUPPORT the two-pathway hypothesis because they demonstrate that implicit and explicit ToM can be independently impaired across development, clinical conditions, and species. A single-system account cannot explain why the same individual shows ceiling performance on one measure and chance performance on another testing the same underlying capacity.

PRACTICE Q3: Moral-Conventional Distinction (Alternative to Kohlberg)
"Compare rationalist (Kohlberg) and sentimentalist approaches to moral development. How does the moral-conventional distinction challenge Kohlberg's stage theory?" (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS Kohlberg's rationalist approach measures moral reasoning development through hierarchical stages, while sentimentalist approaches (Turiel, Smetana, Blair) emphasize early-emerging intuitive distinctions. The moral-conventional distinction fundamentally challenges Kohlberg by demonstrating sophisticated moral understanding years before "conventional" reasoning supposedly emerges.

EVIDENCE - Kohlberg: Six stages across three levels (pre-conventional, conventional, post-conventional). Children progress through reasoning about what one "should" do. Most adults remain at Stages 3-4; post-conventional reasoning is rare (10-15% adults).

EVIDENCE - Moral-Conventional: Smetana (1981) showed 30-month-olds distinguish moral transgressions (hitting = wrong everywhere, even if teacher allows) from conventional transgressions (wearing shoes indoors = OK if teacher allows). Four criteria differentiate domains: authority-independence, rule-independence, generalizability, seriousness.

THE CHALLENGE If 30-month-olds can identify that harm-based transgressions are universally wrong regardless of rules/authority, they possess a form of "moral understanding" that precedes Kohlberg's conventional level by years. The distinction isn't about reasoning sophistication but about domain recognition.

CLINICAL BRIDGE: Blair's VIM studies show children with psychopathic traits have smaller moral-conventional gap (seriousness diff: 1.23 vs 1.84)‚Äîthey treat moral violations more like conventional ones, suggesting deficient Violence Inhibition Mechanism rather than reasoning deficits.

VERDICT These findings CHALLENGE Kohlberg's stage theory because sophisticated moral-conventional discrimination at 30 months contradicts the claim that young children are "pre-conventional" (punishment-oriented only). The sentimentalist critique suggests moral behavior emerges from emotional responses to harm, not cognitive development through stages. However, INTEGRATION is required: reasoning without emotion produces empty principles; intuition without reasoning cannot handle complex dilemmas.

PRACTICE Q4: Nativism vs Neoconstructivism (Core exam topic)
"How does the cognitive overload signature pattern support neoconstructivism over nativism in explaining infant relational learning?" (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS The central empirical test distinguishing nativism from neoconstructivism is cognitive load manipulation. Nativism predicts that once triggered, modules should fire consistently regardless of stimulus complexity. Neoconstructivism predicts load-dependent performance because relational processing depends hierarchically on object processing.

Nativism (Carey, Spelke, Leslie)
‚Ä¢ Domain-specific innate modules
‚Ä¢ Specific inputs trigger modules
‚Ä¢ Should be robust across conditions
Neoconstructivism (Cohen, Oakes)
‚Ä¢ Domain-general statistical learning
‚Ä¢ Hierarchical construction
‚Ä¢ Load-dependent performance

EVIDENCE 1 - Causal Perception: Leslie (1984): 6-month-olds perceive causality with simple geometric shapes. Oakes & Cohen (1990): Same 6-month-olds FAIL with complex realistic toys; 10-month-olds fail with variable objects. STATS Same spatiotemporal cues, different outcomes based on object complexity alone.

EVIDENCE 2 - Same/Different: Anderson et al. (2018): 3-month-olds succeed with 2 pairs, fail with 6 pairs. Ferry et al. (2015): 7-9 month-olds succeed with 4 diverse pairs but fail with familiar-not-habituated objects (salience blocks relational processing).

THE PATTERN Relational success = f(Capacityage ‚àí Loadcognitive). When object processing exceeds capacity (complex objects, high variability, too many exemplars, excessive salience), infants revert from relational to featural processing. This is the Goldilocks principle: not too few (can't extract pattern), not too many (overload), just right.

VERDICT These findings SUPPORT NEOCONSTRUCTIVISM because the same infant at the same age succeeds or fails based on cognitive load alone‚Äîa graded, task-dependent pattern that innate modules cannot explain. If a causal perception module were triggered by spatiotemporal contact + immediate succession, it should fire regardless of whether the objects are simple shapes or complex toys. NATIVISM CANNOT EXPLAIN why load manipulation produces systematic variation when the triggering input remains constant.

"This MISALIGNS with nativist modularity because modules should be informationally encapsulated‚Äîonce triggered, they should operate consistently. The load-dependent pattern reveals that relational processing is not a specialized module but a higher level in a hierarchical system that becomes inaccessible when lower-level object processing exceeds capacity."

PRACTICE Q5: Language Acquisition Debate (High probability)
"Evaluate the poverty of the stimulus argument. How do constructionist accounts explain language acquisition without innate Universal Grammar?" (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS The poverty of stimulus argument claims children acquire syntactic knowledge absent from input (no negative evidence for island constraints), therefore requiring innate Universal Grammar. Constructionists counter that the input is richer than nativists acknowledge and domain-general statistical learning suffices.

NATIVIST EVIDENCE: Children never produce structural dependence errors (e.g., "*Is the boy who smoking is crazy?"). Crain & Nakayama (1987): 600+ questions from 3-5 year-olds showed 0% linear-order errors. Nicaraguan Sign Language: Children systematized deficient adult input within sensitive period, adding compositional structure adults lacked.

CONSTRUCTIONIST COUNTER:

  • Saffran et al. (1996): 8-month-olds segment speech via transitional probabilities after 2 minutes. Within-word TP = 1.0, boundary TP = 0.33.
  • Mintz (2003): Frequent frames ("the ___ is") predict grammatical categories with 87% accuracy.
  • Ambridge, Rowland & Pine (2008): 7-year-olds DO make structural dependence errors when TPs support them‚Äî7% errors with "can" (high frequency in ambiguous positions) vs 0% with "is".

GRADUAL ABSTRACTION Tomasello & Brooks (1998): Novel verb studies show verb-specific, not verb-general syntax. Age 2;0: only 19% produce transitive with novel verb; Age 4: ~90%. If UG provides abstract transitive schema, why can't 2-year-olds extend novel verbs?

VERDICT The evidence PARTIALLY SUPPORTS BOTH POSITIONS. Nativists are correct that children acquire constraints without negative evidence, but constructionists demonstrate: (1) input is richer than claimed (distributional statistics provide substantial information), (2) errors DO occur when statistics support them (undermining absolute UG constraints), (3) abstraction is gradual, not present from onset. The resolution may be that humans have innate domain-GENERAL learning mechanisms (statistical learning, analogy) rather than domain-SPECIFIC syntactic modules‚Äîwhat's innate is the capacity to learn, not the grammar itself.

PRACTICE Q6: Executive Function Development
"Explain the DCCS task paradigm and what it reveals about the competence-performance distinction in executive function development." (10 marks)

‚úÖ MODEL ANSWER

THESIS The Dimensional Change Card Sort (DCCS) demonstrates a striking competence-performance dissociation: 3-year-olds can verbally state the new rule but cannot execute it, revealing that task failure reflects cognitive flexibility limitations, not comprehension deficits.

DCCS PROCEDURE:

PhaseRule3yo Performance5yo Performance
Pre-switch (Shape Game)Sort by shape (ignore color)100% correct100% correct
Rule Check"Where do RED ones go?"Points CORRECTLYPoints correctly
Post-switch (Color Game)Sort by color (ignore shape)0% correct85-95% correct

THE PARADOX Children can STATE the rule perfectly but cannot EXECUTE it. They perseverate on the old dimension despite knowing the new rule. This is NOT a failure of: attention (succeed in pre-switch), memory (state rule correctly), comprehension (understand task), or perception (can see both dimensions).

ACTUAL FAILURE:

  • Cognitive Flexibility: Cannot disconnect established S-R mapping
  • Goal Representation: Cannot maintain new rule representation to guide behavior
  • PFC Maturity: Immature prefrontal cortex cannot reconfigure behavior according to new rule

ALTERNATIVE INTERPRETATIONS: Halford/Andrews: Really about working memory‚Äîmust bind color + shape simultaneously (binary relational complexity). Under-5s fail because this exceeds their relational capacity.

VERDICT DCCS demonstrates that the competence-performance gap is a GENUINE DEVELOPMENTAL PHENOMENON, not just a measurement artifact. Three-year-olds possess the knowledge but lack the executive capacity to deploy it flexibly. This pattern maps onto implicit-explicit dissociations across domains: knowledge can exist in forms that are inaccessible to behavioral control. The theoretical implication is that development involves not just acquiring knowledge but developing the executive architecture to use it flexibly.
