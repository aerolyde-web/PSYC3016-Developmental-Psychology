üìù FULL MODEL ANSWER: "Evaluate Vygotsky's Theory Using Evidence from L14-L26"

THESIS Vygotsky's sociocultural theory receives substantial empirical support from L14-L26 evidence, particularly regarding language as a psychological tool and the Zone of Proximal Development, though with important qualifications about universal cognitive foundations.

EVIDENCE 1: ZPD & SCAFFOLDING Tools of the Mind (Diamond et al., 2007) demonstrates Vygotskian principles operationalized: external scaffolds (ear/lips pictures) enable performance beyond current capacity, with gradual internalization. Results showed Hearts & Flowers mixed accuracy of 72% vs 50% for control‚Äîand critically, control schools abandoned their curriculum because Tools classrooms had far better behavior. This exemplifies Vygotsky's claim that learning LEADS development.

EVIDENCE 2: LANGUAGE AS TOOL The SES gap findings strongly support Vygotsky: low-SES and high-SES children show equivalent mathematical play behaviors but significant gaps in verbal mathematical reasoning. The mechanism is language-mediated reflection‚Äîhigh-SES homes provide richer "why/how" prompts that transform implicit procedural knowledge into explicit, portable concepts. Further, Harris (2001) showed that simply adding "Imagine if..." enables 4-6yo to succeed at syllogistic reasoning they otherwise fail, demonstrating that cultural-linguistic tools reorganize thinking.

EVIDENCE 3: PRIVATE SPEECH Vygotsky's reinterpretation of egocentric speech as functional private speech (not immaturity) is supported by the finding that such speech increases with task difficulty‚Äîchildren externalize when cognitive demands exceed current inner speech capacity, exactly as Vygotsky predicted.

QUALIFICATIONS However, Harris's findings also contradict Vygotsky's collaborators' claim that abstract reasoning requires formal schooling‚Äîcompetence exists pre-school, needing only appropriate framing to access. Additionally, Siegler's targeted games work with minimal social interaction, suggesting representational structure matters independently of social mediation.

SYNTHESIS The resolution: competence exists universally (against strong Vygotskian cultural determinism), BUT language and cultural practices provide essential tools to ACCESS, FORMALIZE, and GENERALIZE that competence. Play reveals latent abilities; education provides the cultural-linguistic scaffolding to make them explicit and transferable.
