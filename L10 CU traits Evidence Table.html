üìù SAQ: "How does the differential amygdala model explain distinct pathways to antisocial behavior?"

Opening The differential amygdala model resolves contradictory findings by distinguishing two functionally distinct nuclei with different roles in fear processing.
BLA Pathway The Basolateral Amygdala (BLA) functions as a stimulus-value learning system. Blair (2007) demonstrates CU youth show ~75% reduction in BLA activation to fearful faces, impairing acquisition of conditioned fear and association of distress cues with negative value. This explains why punishment-based discipline fails ‚Äî their BLA cannot encode aversive significance.
CeA Pathway The Central Amygdala (CeA) generates fear output via hypothalamic projections. CeA hyperactivity characterizes Hot CD, producing excessive fear responses manifesting as reactive aggression. Crucially, CeA remains intact in CU ‚Äî explaining normal unconditioned fear and subjective fear reports.
Clinical Integration Dadds et al. (2006) provides key evidence: CU children's fear recognition jumps from 45% to 78% when instructed to "look at the eyes" ‚Äî attention allocation deficit, not emotion blindness. Newman et al. (2010) confirms: directing attention normalizes conditioned fear from 10‚Äì20% to 35‚Äì50%.
Closing The model has treatment implications: Hot CD requires CeA downregulation (emotion regulation training), while CU requires strengthening BLA stimulus-value learning through explicit, repeated pairing of behaviors with emotional consequences. Same diagnosis, different mechanism, different intervention.

üìù SAQ: "Compare the empathy profiles of CU traits and ASD"

Opening CU traits and ASD represent opposite empathy dissociations with parallel underlying architecture ‚Äî both show implicit/automatic impairment with explicit/conscious compensation.
CU Profile CU youth show intact cognitive empathy (can pass mentalising/ToM tasks) but impaired affective empathy (low guilt/shame, blunted distress response). They "know how you feel, but don't care." Implicit affective system offline, explicit reasoning intact when cued.
ASD Profile ASD shows the reverse: impaired cognitive empathy (struggle to infer mental states) but intact affective empathy (appropriate emotional response once they understand). They "don't know how you feel, but would care if they did." Senju (2009): HF-ASD adults show 100% explicit FB but ~50% implicit (chance).
Architectural Parallel Both conditions show implicit deficit + explicit compensation. HF-ASD: implicit ToM‚Äì / explicit ToM+. CU: implicit empathy‚Äì / explicit empathy+. Both reach "right answers" via slow, effortful PFC routes while fast automatic routes fail ‚Äî same architecture, different domain.
