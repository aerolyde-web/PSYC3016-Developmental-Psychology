Peg Release Paradigm
Within-Species Helping		 Chimpanzee studies Adult chimps Subject can release peg allowing recipient chimp food access; manipulate food location (target vs distractor room) Selective helping based on recipient's goal; incurs time/effort cost High peg release when food in target room; low release for distractor Understands recipient's goal; provides appropriate assistance; eliminates human-chimp relationship confound "Chimps help conspecifics selectively: goal-directed, not random"
Cognitive vs Affective Empathy Conceptual distinction Framework Affective: same emotion as target; Cognitive: appropriate response (may differ) Response appropriateness is the key differentiator Example: Encouraging smile to nervous presenter (cognitive) vs mirroring anxiety (affective) Cognitive = perspective-taking + understanding needs; Affective = automatic mirroring "Affective mirrors emotions; cognitive understands needs"
Cognitive Empathy Summary: Warneken paradigm shows 18-month-olds AND adult chimps help at 40-60% rates when experimenter reaches, dropping to 5-20% without reaching. The 74% altruistic specificity confirms this is genuine prosocial motivation, not reward-driven or random. Four-part decision tree validates altruism: contingency, reward-independence, stranger-generalization, and cost. Peg release paradigm extends to conspecific helping, eliminating cross-species confounds.
