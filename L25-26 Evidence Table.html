L25-L26: Culture & Conceptual Development

Master Evidence Table with Cross-Lecture Connections

Core Thesis: Cultural value systems (individualism-collectivism, human-nature relations) are realized strategies that shape moment-to-moment cognitive operations: attention allocation, memory encoding, relational reasoning, learning mechanisms, and biological categorization. Western psychological theories, when applied to non-Western populations without modification, systematically misidentify cultural differences in cognitive strengths as deficits.

Simultaneous vs Alternating Attention (Origami) Correa-Chávez, Rogoff et al. Mayan children (traditional vs mixed vs European American) Observe children during origami instruction. Mixed-exposure: 35%. European American: 27%. Simultaneous attention correlates negatively with question-asking—superior observational learning. Traditional vs European: 48% - 27% = 21 percentage-point difference. Gradient shows cultural practice exposure effect, not essential ethnicity. Aboriginal Narrative-Place Memory Method Reser et al. (2021) Medical students memorizing butterfly names Compare: (1) Untrained, (2) Western "memory palace" (visualize familiar room), (3) Aboriginal technique (physically walk real place; construct narrative connecting items to landscape; practice with Aboriginal educator guidance). Untrained: 24% perfect recall. Memory palace: 46%. Aboriginal narrative-place: 64%. Superiority from: (1) actual physical movement, (2) narrative structure, (3) connection to real land (not imagined furniture). Aboriginal method: 64% vs 46% = 18 percentage-point advantage (39% relative improvement). Effect size d ≈ 1.5. ALL learners can benefit, not just Indigenous. Cross-Lecture Synthesis: How L25-L26 Connects to Major Course Threads

Thread 2: The Great Debate (Nativist vs Constructivist vs Socio-Cultural)

L25-L26 contribution: Provides the strongest evidence for socio-cultural perspective. Cultural values (individualism-collectivism, human-nature relations) are transmitted and shape cognitive architecture. This challenges both nativist claims (cognition is pre-wired) and constructivist claims (cognition develops through universal individual exploration). The Menominee inductive asymmetry data directly challenges Susan Carey's (1985) claim of universal anthropocentric folk biology—showing that what Piaget and Carey observed was Western-specific, not universal.

Connect to: L14 (Piaget's universalism challenged), L16 (Nativism vs Constructivism), L22-24 (Language as culturally-shaped tool for thought)

Thread 3: EF as Master Predictor

L25-L26 contribution: Shows that attention allocation—often studied as EF component—is culturally trained. Japanese children's relational focus and Mayan children's simultaneous attention are not "better EF" but different strategic deployment shaped by cultural values. Implications: EF interventions must consider cultural context; what looks like "EF deficit" may be strategic mismatch.

Connect to: L21 (DCCS shows flexibility, not knowledge deficit—same logic here), L19-20 (relational reasoning and play)

Thread 4: Human Uniqueness

L25-L26 contribution: Intent participation is an alternative to the "active teaching" pathway emphasized in human uniqueness literature. Humans are unique in active teaching (L05-07), but some cultures emphasize learning through observation more than explicit instruction. Both pathways transmit culture—just differently. The factory model's explicit instruction emphasis is recent historical invention, not human default.

Connect to: L05-07 (shared intentionality, active teaching), L12 (adult-child interaction patterns)

Thread 5: Developmental Timing

L25-L26 contribution: Factory model schooling creates artificial developmental trajectories (segregation from adult activity until age 18+). Indigenous intent participation shows alternative: children participate in meaningful adult work from early childhood. This connects to maturity gap (L11)—industrial societies extended "childhood" and created gap between biological and social maturity.

Connect to: L11 (maturity gap 0yr→7yr since 1920s), L13 (juvenile justice assumes factory-model development)

Thread 6: ToM as Hub

L25-L26 contribution: Perspective-taking measured in diorama play (Native 88% vs European 41% embody animal perspective) shows ToM/perspective-taking is culturally variable. If "part of nature" mental model, perspective-taking extends to non-humans. This challenges studies measuring ToM with only human targets.

Connect to: L05-07 (ToM development), L20 (dual representation in play)

Bottom Line for SAQs: When to Use L25-L26 Evidence

  1. Theory Evaluation questions about universality: Use Medin/Kuwabara to argue Piaget/Carey's "universal" findings were actually Western-specific
  2. Species Comparison questions: Use intent participation to argue human teaching isn't monolithic—observation vs explicit instruction pathways
  3. Applied/Intervention questions: Use Gawura/self-concept reciprocal to argue for strength-based framing over deficit model
  4. Theory Critique of Attachment Theory: Use child welfare misapplication as example of cross-cultural theory harm
  5. Debate questions (Nativist vs Constructivist vs Socio-Cultural): Use all L25-L26 evidence to argue for socio-cultural perspective
