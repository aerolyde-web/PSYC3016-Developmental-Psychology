1. CALLOUS-UNEMOTIONAL TRAITS: Core Thesis (L10)

  • CU Traits: Reduced empathy, low guilt/shame, limited prosocial emotions, reduced affect. NOT absent empathy—cognitive empathy INTACT, affective empathy IMPAIRED.
  • Core Mechanism: Impaired automatic attention allocation (underactive BLA) → miss social learning opportunities → cascading developmental failures → antisocial behavior.
  • Critical Insight: Subtle cognitive deficits present from birth snowball through development. Explicit processing INTACT; implicit/automatic processing IMPAIRED. "200ms slower × 1000s daily × 365 days × 10 yrs = massive deficit."
  • DSM-5: "Limited Prosocial Emotions" specifier for Conduct Disorder—avoids "psychopathy" label for children.
CU = automatic attention allocation failure (BLA), not absent empathy. Cognitive empathy INTACT, affective IMPAIRED. Subtle deficits snowball. DSM-5 = "Limited Prosocial Emotions."

5. ADOLESCENT DEVELOPMENT: Fundamental Asynchrony (L11)

  • Central Thesis: Adolescence = temporal mismatch: (1) Hormones (Tanner-linked, peaks early), (2) Cognitive control (age-linked, matures late), (3) Peer influence (peaks mid-adolescence).
  • Three Stages: Early (10-15): Max mismatch, adult hormones + child cognition. Middle (14-17): PEAK peer influence, authority challenges. Late (16-19): Integrating, PFC still to 25.
  • The Paradox: Age 15 = adult-level hypothetical risk assessment, but 2-3× higher real-world risk-taking. Conformity: 76% age 14 → 42% age 18 → 28% adult.
  • "Powerful engine, unlicensed driver": Engine full power by 14. Driver learning until 25. Ratio at 14 ≈ 2.9:1. Moffitt: Adolescent-limited antisocial = rational response to maturity gap.
3 stages: Early (max mismatch), Middle (peak peers), Late (integrating). Paradox: cold intact by 15, hot fails. Conformity: 76%→42%→28%. Engine:Driver = 2.9:1. Moffitt: rational response to gap.
