LO1-D The Adolescent Paradox: Capable Reasoning, Risky Actions
"Explain the adolescent paradox and why adolescents engage in risky behavior despite understanding the risks..."
# Concept Key Evidence ‚Üí SAQ Function
1 The Paradox Defined By age 15, adolescents MATCH adults in hypothetical risk assessment. Yet show 200-300% higher real-world risk-taking (unprotected sex, dangerous driving, substance use) Establishes puzzle
2 Cold Cognition
Hypothetical		 Non-emotional, hypothetical contexts. Risk perception, probability estimation, consequence evaluation ‚Äî NO reliable age differences after middle adolescence KNOWING is intact
3 Hot Cognition
Real-World		 Emotionally-charged, real-world contexts. Limbic activation overwhelms PFC. Arousal, peer presence, reward proximity amplify drives DOING fails
4 Resolution Not ignorance or cognitive deficit. Fundamental mismatch between cognitive capacity (knowing) and regulatory control (doing). Knowledge intact; implementation capacity neurobiologically limited Explains paradox
COLD Cognition: Hypothetical, non-emotional ‚Üí PFC handles ‚Üí No age differences after 15
HOT Cognition: Real-world, emotional ‚Üí Limbic overwhelms PFC ‚Üí Adolescents fail despite knowing

‚ö° INTERVENTION IMPLICATIONS

Single-system interventions FAIL. High testosterone + immature PFC + peer pressure = multiplicative vulnerability.
Multi-system approach: (1) Biological: optimize sleep, nutrition, exercise. (2) Cognitive: scaffold EF through structure. (3) Social: engineer positive peer contexts, adult mentorship. Education alone fails because knowledge is INTACT ‚Äî implementation capacity is the limit.
