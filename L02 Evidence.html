SNP (Single Nucleotide Polymorphism) Molecular genetics methodology All individuals; DNA analysis Identify locations where DNA differs by one nucleotide. Big A = common allele; little a = rare allele. Genotypes: AA, Aa, aa. SNP genotyping: At each SNP location, three possibilities: AA (homozygous common), Aa (heterozygous), aa (homozygous rare). Compare frequencies between affected vs unaffected groups. Homozygous = two same; Heterozygous = one of each SNPs are the building blocks of genetic association studies. Each SNP = tiny contribution to complex traits. "At each DNA spot: two commons, one of each, or two rares â€” that's the genetic menu."
GWAS (Genome-Wide Association Study) Molecular genetics methodology Large samples (thousands-millions) Compare SNP frequencies across millions of locations between cases (with disorder) and controls (without). Statistical association. Method: Collect DNA (cheek swab/saliva), genotype millions of SNPs, compare frequencies. If one SNP significantly more common in cases â†’ associated with disorder. Association â‰  causation. Requires N > 10,000 for power; tests millions of SNPs; Bonferroni correction for multiple testing Problem: GWAS often identifies random DNA regions with unknown function. "Fishing trip" without hypothesis. "GWAS scans millions of DNA spots â€” finds associations but often not causes."
