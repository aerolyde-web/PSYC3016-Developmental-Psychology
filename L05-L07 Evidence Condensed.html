L05-L07: Social Cognition & Theory of Mind – Evidence Table

study / concept sample / age key numbers / pattern exam hook / tag
L05: SELF-RECOGNITION & EMPATHY
Rouge Test
Amsterdam (1972)		 18-36mo; great apes ~50% pass at 24mo; ~90% by 36mo; chimps 75%, orangutans 60%, gorillas 30% Objective self-awareness; same capacity in apes; creates "understand others before self" paradox
Contagious Crying
Affective empathy		 Newborn Cry more/longer to other infant cries vs neutral sounds; pure emotional resonance "Cry when others cry" – earliest affective empathy from birth
Contagious Yawning
Campbell & de Waal (2011)		 Adult chimps Familiar: ~3.0/session; unfamiliar: ~1.5; in-group: 3.1 vs out-group: 0.3; geladas: 0.2 Social & safety-gated contagion – "15× more yawning for friends"
Affective vs Cognitive Empathy
de Waal (2008)		 Framework Affective = mirroring emotion; Cognitive = understanding needs. Dissociable pathways Psychopathy = intact cognitive, impaired affective; ASD may show opposite
Altruistic Helping
Warneken & Tomasello (2006)		 18mo; chimps Reaching: 40-60% help; no-reaching: 5-20%; 74% reward-independent Helping contingent on goal/need, not reward – cognitive empathy in action
L06: TRIADIC INTERACTIONS → IMPLICIT FALSE BELIEF
Teleological Reasoning
Gergely & Csibra (1995/99)		 6mo & 9mo 9mo: ~42s irrational vs ~18s rational jump (~5× longer); 6mo track goals but not efficiency Expect efficient actions toward goals; teleology precedes ToM
Joint Attention
Carpenter et al. (1998)		 9-15mo Gaze alternation increases 9→15mo; predicts language (r=.52); episode: 4.2s→18.6s Shift from dyadic to triadic – "looking together knowingly"
Declarative vs Imperative Pointing
Bates (1979)		 9-12mo Imperative (~9mo): "give me" + few checking looks; Declarative (~12mo): "look at that" + frequent checking Declarative = motivation to share mental states, not just get objects
Social Referencing
Sorce et al. (1985)		 ~12mo Visual cliff: 74% cross with happy mother; 0% with fear expression "Is it safe, mum?" – use others' emotions as information
Selective Informing
Liszkowski et al. (2007)		 12-14mo Point 2.4× more when adult missed event (M=1.27 vs 0.53) Early perception→knowledge link – "you didn't see it!"
Implicit FB – Watermelon
Onishi & Baillargeon (2005)		 15mo FB-inconsistent: 39.3s; FB-consistent: 29.1s; Δ=10.2s Implicit FB at 15mo – 2.5yrs before explicit Sally-Anne
Anticipatory Looking FB
Southgate et al. (2007)		 25mo First saccade to FB-location 1-2yrs before verbal success "Eyes know before mouth" – implicit online before explicit
L07: EXPLICIT ToM, APES, & CLINICAL POPULATIONS
Sally-Anne FB
Baron-Cohen et al. (1985)		 3-4yr; ASD; DS 3yr: ~15%; 4yr: ~85%; ASD: 20%; DS: ~86% Explicit FB ~4yrs; requires dual representation + inhibition
EF × False Belief
Carlson & Moses (2001)		 3-5yr Inhibitory control correlation: r=0.66 EF is the cognitive gatekeeper for explicit ToM
Chimp Food Competition
Bräuer et al. (2007)		 Subordinate vs dominant chimps Hidden food: 42% retrieval; visible: 27%; FB: 33% (chance) Track perceptual access/ignorance but fail false belief
Ape Eye-Tracking
Krupenye et al. (2016)		 Great apes Anticipatory looking: ~70% to FB location; explicit behavioral: 0% Implicit ToM phylogenetically ancient; explicit human-unique
Shared Intentionality
Tomasello (2005)		 Human children vs apes Children maintain joint goal; apes defect for individual reward "We-mode" human-unique; basis of culture & cooperation
HF-ASD Adolescents
Scheeren et al. (2013)		 n=194 HF-ASD vs TD No sig. diff. on 2nd-order FB, faux pas, sarcasm by adolescence Explicit ToM DELAYED not ABSENT – compensatory learning
HF-ASD Eye-Tracking
Senju et al. (2009)		 HF-ASD adults (IQ≈115) Explicit: 100%; Implicit (anticipatory): ~50% (chance) Pathway dissociation: "right answer, wrong process"
Two-Pathway Architecture
Apperly & Butterfill		 Framework Implicit: 150-300ms, TPJ, 9mo, apes. Explicit: 2000-3000ms, PFC, 4yr, human Double dissociation: Apes (I+/E−) vs HF-ASD (I−/E+) = independent systems
