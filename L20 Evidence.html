Schulz & Bonawitz (2007): Confounded Evidence Condition Schulz & Bonawitz (2007) Preschool age Jack-in-box with 2 levers, 2 pop-ups. Confounded condition: Adult and child pull both levers simultaneously → both toys pop up. Then offer choice: continue playing with old toy OR explore novel toy 65% choose old toy to continue playing. Causal structure unresolved—ambiguity remains (P(dark→bird) = P(light→bird) = 0.5). Children preferentially continue playing to resolve causal ambiguity 65% choose old toy in confounded condition; P(dark→bird) = P(light→bird) = 0.5 (causal ambiguity); play duration inversely correlates with causal certainty Gopnik Play is motivated by drive to resolve causal ambiguity (information-seeking), not merely novelty or hedonic value "Confounded jack-in-box (both levers pulled simultaneously) → 65% continue playing with old toy to resolve causal ambiguity—play duration ∝ causal uncertainty"
