L08: RATIONALIST TRADITION (KOHLBERG) & CRITIQUES
Kohlberg's 6-Stage Model
Kohlberg (1958-1984)		 10yr→adult; males only Pre-conventional (S1-2): punishment/self-interest; Conventional (S3-4): most adults; Post-conventional S5: 10-15%; S6: ~1% Measures reasoning/justification, not answer; "what SHOULD be done?" not "is it right?"
Gilligan Gender Bias
Gilligan (1982)		 Re-analysis of male sample Ethics of care (relationships) vs justice (rules); S3 (care) scored lower than S4 = systematic bias "Different voice, not deficient" – two orientations, not hierarchy
Cultural Bias Critique Cross-cultural adults Collectivist cultures reverse hierarchy: interpersonal harmony (S3) > abstract justice (S4) Kohlberg reflects WEIRD values; Japanese prioritize relationships over legal technicalities
Reasoning-Behavior Gap
Blasi (1980) meta-analysis		 Studies across ages Reasoning → behavior correlation: r = 0.30-0.40 (weak) "Talk is cheap" – knowing ≠ doing; emotion + motivation required
L08: MORAL-CONVENTIONAL DISTINCTION (TURIEL)
Social Domain Theory
Turiel (1983)		 All ages; framework Moral = harm-based, universal, unalterable; Conventional = rule-based, context-dependent, authority-modifiable "Hitting vs dress code" – harm is the switch that converts convention to moral
M/C Distinction Study
Smetana (1981)		 30-57mo (2.5-4.75yr) NO age differences; even 30mo rate moral violations as more serious, wrong everywhere, wrong without rule Earliest moral cognition at 2.5yr – challenges stage theory
Reasoning vs Appraisal
Theoretical framework		 Different ages Reasoning (Kohlberg): "what SHOULD be done?" = adolescence; Appraisal (Turiel): "is it right?" = 30mo Different questions, different ages; appraisal (sentimentalist) precedes reasoning (rationalist)
L09: SENTIMENTALIST TRADITION (BLAIR VIM)
Violence Inhibition Mechanism
Blair (2005)		 Theoretical model Distress cues (fear/sadness) > threshold → withdrawal → negative reinforcement; ΔR = -0.2/episode; 10 episodes = -2.0 units VIM teaches morality through emotional conditioning, not explicit reasoning
VIM: Emotion Recognition
Blair (2005)		 High vs low PSD children Fear/sadness errors: high PSD = 4.73 vs low = 2.20 (2.14× more); normal for happiness, anger Selective distress-cue deficit – not general emotion blindness
VIM: M/C Distinction
Blair (1997/2005)		 High vs low PSD children M/C seriousness gap: high PSD = 1.23 vs low = 1.84 (33% smaller); high PSD treat moral as conventional Impaired VIM → treat hitting as "OK if no rule" – rules replace feelings
VIM: Predictive Power
Blair (2005)		 M/C pass vs fail M/C failers = 21.95 PSD vs passers = 18.39 (Δ = 3.56); strongest on motivation/impulsivity M/C failure predicts psychopathic traits; validates emotion-morality link
Blair VIM Revision
(EXAM TRAP!)		 Later research Children with psychopathic traits CAN feel empathy under certain conditions; deficit = response modulation VIM doesn't explain ALL antisocial behavior – recognition vs response distinction
Darwin Dual-Instinct
Theoretical framework		 Framework Social instincts: constant, moderate; Appetites: sudden, strong; guilt = dissatisfaction when S > A after transgression Post-transgression: appetite fades, social instinct persists → "why did I do that?" = guilt
L09: INTENTION-OUTCOME & HAPPY VICTIMIZER
Piaget Intention-Outcome
Piaget (1932)		 5-10yr John breaks 15 cups accidentally vs Henry breaks 1 cup intentionally; 5-7yr: "John naughtier" (outcome); 8-10yr: "Henry" (intention) BUT single comparison confounds intent/outcome – need factorial design
Water Hose Study
Heyman & Gelman (1998)		 Kindy vs adults; 2×2 factorial Kindy: outcome effect = 5.0, intention = 1.0 → 5:1 ratio; Adults: intention = 4.0, outcome = 1.0 → 4:1 reversed Complete reversal in weighting; children use BOTH but overweight outcome
Weighting Formula
Developmental model		 Model Judgment = α×Intention + β×Outcome; Age 5: α=0.3, β=0.7; Age 8: α=0.6, β=0.4; Adults: α=0.8, β=0.2 Continuous reweighting, not categorical shift: β/α=2.33 → α/β=4.0
Happy Victimizer
Nunner-Winkler & Sodian (1988)		 4-8yr Q1 "wrong?" = YES all ages; Q2 "how feel?": age 4 = "happy" (+1.35); age 8 = "guilty" (−0.50); shift = 1.85 units Moral knowledge precedes emotional binding; 4yr = "motivational honesty"
HV Priming Effects 5-7yr (transitional) Moral frame first → ↑guilt; Desire frame first → ↑HV responses Competing systems not yet integrated; attention shifts response
L09: PERSONALITY ATTRIBUTION & META-ANALYSIS
Personality: Separate
Ages 4-5		 4-5yr Can do B→T OR T→B but cannot chain B→T→B; update: 0.1×previous + 0.9×current; reset ≈ 3 days "Daily relationship resets" – each interaction fresh; fluid social world
Personality: Integrated
Ages 7-8		 7-8yr B→T→B chains automatic; update: 0.6×previous + 0.4×current; reset ≈ 45 days (15× longer) Stable reputations form; intervention window closes by 7-8
Moral Emotion Meta-Analysis
Malti & Krettenauer (2013)		 42 studies, >8000; ages 4-20 Emotion → antisocial: r = −0.35; Emotion → prosocial: r = +0.20; asymmetry = 1.75× Moral emotions inhibit bad > motivate good; "guilt stops bad more than starts good"
