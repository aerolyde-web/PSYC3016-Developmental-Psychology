L02: NATURE-NURTURE FRAMEWORK
Phenotype Equation
Core framework		 All traits P = G + E. Phenotype = Genetic effects + Environmental effects. All complex traits heritable (~50% typical) NOT "nature vs nurture" but "how do they interact?" – debate is over
Heritability Definition
Critical distinction		 Population statistic h² = proportion of variance in population due to genetic differences. NOT individual composition, NOT determinism "h²=0.60 ≠ 60% of YOUR trait is genetic" – ecological fallacy
SES & Brain Development
Farah (referenced)		 High vs low SES; birth→12yr At birth: identical brain structures. By age 12: poverty reduced hippocampal + PFC volumes Environment causes biological brain differences – not just genes
CD Heritability by CU Traits Children with CD Low CU: h²=0.30, PMT success 60%. High CU: h²=0.81, PMT success 20% Same diagnosis, 51-point h² gap, 3× treatment diff – h² is clinically actionable
L03: TWIN STUDIES & ACE MODEL
Twin Study Logic
Naturalistic experiment		 MZ vs DZ twins MZ: 100% genes, 100% common env. DZ: 50% genes, 100% common env. If MZ > DZ similarity → genetic influence "Nature's perfect experiment" – same home, different genes
Falconer's Formula Derivation r_MZ = h² + c²; r_DZ = ½h² + c². h² = 2(r_MZ − r_DZ). Common env cancels algebraically Memorize: h² = 2 × difference in twin correlations
ACE Model
Variance decomposition		 Framework A = additive genetic; C = common/shared env; E = unique env + error. Total = A + C + E = 1.0 When h² > 1.0 → use ADE model (dominance effects)
Burt (2009) Meta-Analysis
490 studies		 Child psychopathology CD: a²=.50, c²=.14. ODD: c²=.16 (highest). Depression: e²=.42. ADHD exception: c²=0, d²=.44, total h²=.70 Shared env DOES matter (10-15%) except ADHD – justifies parenting interventions
Parenting Effects
Shared environment (C)		 Clinical implication Overprotection → anxiety (validates fear). Hostility → conduct problems. Overcontrol → internalizing "Not bad parents" – protective feels right but has unintended effects
Turkheimer's Paradox Theoretical limit Know h²≈50%, e²≈35%. GWAS finds <10% of genetic variance. Can't specify unique env variables either "Know THAT genes + experiences matter, not WHAT they are"
Twin Study Limitations Critiques EEA violation: MZ treated more similarly → inflates h². Assortative mating: inflates h². Population-specific: can't generalize h² is context-dependent – different populations, different estimates
L04: G×E INTERACTIONS & EPIGENETICS
Missing Heritability Problem GWAS literature Twin h²≈50%. GWAS identifies 2-3%. Gap = ~47% "missing". Causes: polygenic, epistasis, rare variants, G×E "We know it exists but can't find the specific genes"
Mediation vs Moderation Statistical framework Mediation: HOW/WHY (sequential pathway X→M→Y). Moderation: WHEN/FOR WHOM (interaction, boundary condition) G×E = moderation – genotype changes strength of environment effect
Differential Susceptibility
Orchid-Dandelion		 Framework Orchids: high susceptibility allele → worst in bad env, BEST in good env. Dandelions: stable regardless. Lines CROSS (not just diathesis-stress) "For better AND for worse" – not just vulnerability, also plasticity
Fast Track Study
NR3C1 Gene		 High-risk 1st graders → age 25; RCT GG (dandelion): Control 50%, Intervention 50% = NO EFFECT. A carrier (orchid): Control 75%, Intervention 18% = HUGE EFFECT "Orchids: 75%→18% with intervention; Dandelions: 50%→50% no change"
MAOA × Maltreatment
Classic G×E		 Longitudinal MAOA-L + maltreatment → violence. MAOA-L alone: no effect. Maltreatment alone: modest effect. Interaction, not main effects "Neither gene nor environment alone – only together"
Epigenetics
Molecular mechanism		 G↔E interface Methylation: environment changes gene expression (not sequence). Prenatal stress → methylation → altered phenotype lifelong "Environment changes genes; genes select environments" – bidirectional
Nature-Nurture Resolution Course integration L02: P=G+E. L03: h²≈50%, c²≈10-16%. L04: G×E + epigenetics. Question is HOW they interact, not WHETHER "Nature vs nurture is over – they dance together, epigenetics is the music"
